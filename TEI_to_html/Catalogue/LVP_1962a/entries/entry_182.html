(Vimalakīrti-nirdeśa)
Ch. 51. I. 36, Pothī; 64 c. x 9.7 c.: fol. 1, numbered kha 10 (bcu thaM); ll. 6: dbu-can.
Verso, l. 3. colophon of the second chapter: Acintya-upāya-kauśalya nāma... (no mention of the work, but agrees with Bkaḥ-ḥgyur).
Bhagavat, Śāriputra, and the Licchavi Vimalakīrti: 'This your body...born from the Skandhas is subject to the four hundred and four illnesses; the body of the Tathāgatas, being born from their resolutions (adhimukti), from gift, from meditation....'
At the beginning of the third chapter Bhagavat sends Śāriputra to Vimalakīrti.
