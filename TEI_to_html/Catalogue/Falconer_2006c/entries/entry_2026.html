View south from T. IV.a. showing line of wall and camp of 1907 in distance (Kabil on wall). [From near Toghrak-bulak.]
PUBLISHED: 'Innermost Asia', vol. 1, fig. 190: 'North-eastern corner of terminal basin of Su-lo-ho, with lines of limes agger, looking south from T.IV.a.' The watch tower T.IV.a. is located a little south of Toghrak-bulak (I.A. Map 35, C4). 'Fig. 190 shows the reed-covered north-eastern corner of the great marshy basin in which the Su-lo-ho terminates, as seen to the south of T.IV.a. In the foreground, at the foot of the steep clay terrace, there is seen the beginning of the Limes agger, built of reed fascines and stamped clay. The figure of the man standing marks its foot on the inside...Further on, the line of the wall, decayed into a mere low mound and overgrown with reeds, could be made out by the naked eye running straight south towards the large clay terrace on which my Camp 171a of 1907 had stood...' (i, 343, note).

Location:
China; Dunhuang Limes; Toghrak-bulak (Su-lo-ho); Xinjiang
