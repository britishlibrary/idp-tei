Earthwork started on tugh above Tülküch-köl. [At Khanaka-tokai.]
PUBLISHED: 'Innermost Asia', vol. 1, fig. 105: 'Men at work on new barrage across flood-bed above Tulkuch-tarim.' (I.A. Map 19, B1). On his arrival at Tulkuch-kol Stein found that floods of recent years threatened the existence of the small colony, by destroying a barrage of the irrigation canal which brought water to the fields. 'I may mention that my timely return to the Niya site incidentally offered a chance of saving the Tulkuch-tarim. My resumed excavations had brought a comparatively large force of able-bodied labourers near the spot. So when I had returned with them from the ruins and was leaving for Charchan I was glad to set them to work to raise a new tugh across the deep-cut flood-bed at the point where Nurullah's previosu effort had failed....' (i, 141).
