Kala-i-Wamar Fort from river bank, looking north-east.
'The castle in whichthe sons or brothers of the Shughnan Mirs, deputed to govern Roshan, used to reside retains its thick outer walls of rough stonework. They are reinforced by large tree trunks set in it, after a fashion from the Indian NW frontier right away to the Oxus. The interior, badly decayed, showed no distinctive local features.' (ii, 887).

Location:
Kala-i-Wamar; Tajikistan
