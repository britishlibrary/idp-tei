Lai-lai-maio (back court) [at Sun-nai-pao].
View of courtyard and temple. '...A short distance east of the village marked on the map as Sun-nai-pao [in a note Stein casts some doubt on the correctness of this name], we made a night's halt at a large and interesting temple. It is known as Lai-lai-miao (?) and is situated near high dunes. It seemed fairly old and the sand was invading all of its numerous courts and shrines. With its elaborate and graceful wooden pavilions, balustraded passages, and bridges connecting different shrines it recalled more closely the architecture of celestial mansions mansions as represented in the Ch'ien-fo-tung paintings of the Western Paradise than any of the structures seen by me on Chinese ground. Its remains when completely abandoned to the drift-sand may provide an interesting 'site' for some future excavator a thousand years or more hence. It is evidently a cherished place of pilgrimage for the inhabitants of the flourishing walled villages by the [Kan-chou] river, and I regret not to have been able to secure an account of its origin and tradition.' (i, 520-21). (I.A. Map 46, A2).

Location:
China; Sun-nai-pao; Xinjiang
