Stacks of fascines to north of T. XI from south-east (Kabil). [East of Chong-kol.]
PUBLISHED: 'Innermost Asia', vol. 1, fig. 193: 'Petrified stacks of fascines outside Watch-tower T.XI, Tun-huang Limes.' (I.A. Map 35, D4). 'In Figs. 193 and 198 [print 461] we see stacks of reed fascines, more or less petrified through the action of salt, as they rise in rows, quincunx fashion, near the watch-towers T.XI and T.XIII respectively. As fully explained in Serindia, ii. pp. 677 sqq., these regular stacks were primarily intended to keep material conveniently ready for the lighting of signal fires.' (i, 344, note).

Location:
China; Chong-kol; Dunhuang Limes; Xinjiang
