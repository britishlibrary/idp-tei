Ruined dwellings of Kafir Kala, Bidegh [Bidech], from west, with view up towards Sendiw.
View of ruins, with the Shakh-dara river in the background. 'Here at the grazing ground of Bidech, a terrace rising about 80 feet above the riverine jungle is covered over an area of about 150 yards by 120 with massive walls of ruined dwellings, to which tradition ascribes 'Kafir' origin. The masonry of flat unhewn stones set in mud plaster shows considerable solidity and is certainly superior to any seen by me in Shughni buildings...' (ii, 881). PUBLISHED: 'Innermost Asia', vol. 2, fig. 417: 'Ruined dwellings at Bidech, Shakh-dara, Shughnan.'

Location:
Bidech; Tajikistan
