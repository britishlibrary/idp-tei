Fatteh Ali Shah's Kala at Kala-i-Panja, from north (enlarged).
PUBLISHED: 'Innermost Asia', vol. 2, fig. 398: 'Kala-i-Panja, seen from right bank of Ab-i-Panja.' 'Four miles below Zang we passed in full view of Kala-i-Panja, the seat of the Mirs of Wakhan in modern times and now occupied by the chief Afghan post in the valley. Counting only some 15 households and situated by the side of a huge fan of detritus, this 'capital' of Afghan Wakhan looked a very desolate place.' (ii, 865).

Location:
Kala-i-Panja; Tajikistan
