Boats starting from left bank, Indus at Hodar.
View looking down onto a raft made from inflated bullock skins: 'The heat of the summer is great in the deep-cut rock defiles of the Indus, and the banks forbiddingly barren. So I was glad when our descent in the Indus gorge next day could be effected on a skin raft which the tossing current of the mighty river carried down at a rate of some 14 miles an hour...' (A third journey..., p. 5).

Location:
Hodar; India/Pakistan disputed territory; Indus (river); Kashmir
