[Manuscript in the National Library of India, New Delhi: not currently available on IDP.]

In the 5th year, 7th month, 6th day of his majesty the great King Aṃguv́a[ka . . . .], the guśura Viharavala, the [tuǵuj́a] Kapuṃca, the tasuca Rahulatǵa and the kitsa < i > tsa Kuḍ́e (Kutre) [. . .]. Kuv́aya, Moǵata, Cimola, and Lýimiṃna — belonging to these there is a farm [. . .] and arable land and a vineyard inherited from their father and grandfather. So now they have made an equal division of it all (including) the detailed items of clothing, bedding, etc. A decision has therefore been reached. Also this younger brother Cimola has beaten Kuv́aya and broken a bone. Now Cimola has been punished. Seventy blows have been given him, he is much cut up. The recompense has been made a man five diṣṭis high. So from now on the elder brother shall beat the younger brother, and the father shall beat the son — so is an end to be made to this matter. With reference (to this matter), this (letter) has been written to the kitsa < i > tsa Piteya. Whoever of these brothers injures another, they are to be stopped. Also the maravara Lepaya has a dispute with Kuv́aya about a camel. Kuv́aya shall come here.

Note: In line (2) read (as offered alternatively) tuǵuj́a instead of v́uǵaj́a. Tuǵuj́a occurs elsewhere as a title. Read (4) astarana vastarana. Cf. 431 and Lüders, Textilien, p. 32.
