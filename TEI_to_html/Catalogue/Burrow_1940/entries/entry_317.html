Section: Obv.
To the cozbo Soṃjaka of pleasant aspect, dear to men and gods, the cozbo Bhimasena [. . . ] am well here. Whatever business there may be, there is no correct account of it with me the great king. Also [. . . ] also they are sending messengers. Even so you are unwilling, you do not allow [. . . ] should be suitable. These people who administer the royal tax there [. . . ]. The revenue which has been assessed from the town office there [. . . ]. You write here on account of the reckoning. So in protsa kres̱a [. . .] time, so you must come here so that [. . .]

Note: Quite obscure is protsa kres̱a. It might be some place-name.
