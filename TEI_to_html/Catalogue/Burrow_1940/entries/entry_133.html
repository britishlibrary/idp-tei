Section: Obv.
[Manuscript in the National Library of India, New Delhi: not currently available on IDP.]

At the feet of his dear father cozbo Lýipeya, etc. . . Tǵaca rubs his head, etc. . . . and thus (writes): From there you sent a horseman Kunas̱ena to keep watch here. It is true that Oǵaca, an acoviṃna, came here from Saca. He says that the Supis have come to Koǵitasasa; with reference to this fact I have sent Kunas̱ena there (so) another horseman is to be sent here quickly. There is no acoviṃna man from there in the mountains.


Section: Rev.
To his dear neighbour. Svaneya, cozbo Namarazma sends health. Whatever slaves of ours there are, must all be put in the city.

Note: Read parvadeṣu as in n. 2.
