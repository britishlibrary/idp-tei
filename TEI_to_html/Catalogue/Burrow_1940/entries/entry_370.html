Section: Obv.
(To cozbo Soṃjaka).
To the beloved of men and gods, of pleasing, aspect, blessed with a good name, his dear brother the cozbo Soṃjaka, the ogu Alýaya writes asking after his health and well-being, again and again, much, immeasurable. And thus (he says), namely: I am your brother and kinsman [. . . ] on your account. If his majesty [. . . ] with feelings of love.Also the young son has been sent there who from the people [. . . ] giving and taking. By all means let him be under the care of you the cozbo. Also we inform you thus: Some men of Kroraina have stayed in our house. They are debtors to us. Those people have fled there by night. By all means let this person be under your care. As a token of remembrance (I) have sent one hastavarṣaǵa and five [?].

Note: (4) Read probably vasitae instead of asitae, as in note 4; from vas- "to dwell".
