To dear brother cozbo Lýipeya, etc. . . . the tasuca Kunala and Sunaka, etc. . . . thus (write): From there you sent Catona here concerning the water and seed, to do the cultivation. I have read the wedge-tablet here. In this wedge-tablet there is no mention of water and seed. The old people speak thus: The use of a farm was given to the cozbo Lýipeya in Saca, water and seed were not given. According as how the field was received from the feet of his majesty, in such wise it belongs to you. Whatever hand-(written) letter there may be there concerning water and seed, or if there is a letter of command with a detailed account, it is to be looked for and sent here. If there is no such (document) there, the price of the water and seed is to be sent from there and the cultivation will take place here. Also the old people say thus: When Sarpika was settled here he used to provide the land, the people of Saca provided the seed and water, and the katmas did the cultivation.
