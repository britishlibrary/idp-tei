. . . .] the wheat was two and three times watered. This is a register of it. (List follows.)

Note: Goma is the usual form taken by Skt. godhūma in the language of the documents. Other forms are goduma and gohomi. In the beginning of this document for [j́a]huma ([ga]-) read no doubt [go]huma. At the end of the first line pa . . . ǵa presumably represents pravaṃnaǵa, the usual name for lists of this kind (cf. 59). Pita and pitaǵa ("drunk") means "was watered". In another place (703) the causative payita is used.
