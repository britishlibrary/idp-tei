Section: Obv.
To the foot of the master, etc. . . . his dear father the great cozbo Soṃjaka, the scribe (divira) Naṃdas̱ena sends his respects and greetings, much, immeasurable. I am pleased that you are well; I, too, am well here through your favour. This is what I have to say: All is in your divine knowledge. Whichever affairs you leave to me here, I receive them (?). We are sending all the letter-carriers. Also by me there is to be received from the people there, from the monk Saṃgaratha one arnavaj́i, from Suyama (one) felt (namata), from cȧru Jimoya one namata, from the mararara Kuviñeya one namata. By all means command must expressly be given to them, that they should send them here quickly. Also I inform you that rotaṃna (madder) is due (kicamaǵa) to me here. The rotaṃna should be sent here in the hand of the letter-carriers (leha[raǵa]na). (It will be) possible to do you a favour in return.

Notes: (3) uparyaṃ mṛdhena remains obscure. Read paḍ́ichami for pratichami.
(5) Insert avaśa before teṣa piḍ́ita. For ada read eda.
