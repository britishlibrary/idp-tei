Section: Obv.
[Manuscript in the National Library of India, New Delhi: not currently available on IDP.]

In the 7th year the cozbo [. . . . ]. Oǵaca and Sarviga sold land to Kuv́aya. The price was received — one cow in calf. The land (had a capacity for seed) of thirty khi. The witnesses were (a man) called Pugiṣa, a karsenav́a and śramana called Buddhavarma and the accountant Mala[. . .]. They cut the string.

Note: Judging from similiar passages we must read [sutra] chinidati instead of [raǵa] chinidati.
