A. (1) Namarasma's parcel was four milima ten khi, etc., etc.

Note: Of the commodities mentioned in the list a number are still obscure.
oǵana is some agricultural product measured in milima and khi.
aǵiṣǵa should be read aǵiṣḍha as suggested in the Inde Verborum (cf. also Lüders, Textilien im alten Turkestan, p. 12). It is probably some kind of rug or blanket.
ṣoṣami is something numbered, not measured, but there is no indication as to its meaning.
