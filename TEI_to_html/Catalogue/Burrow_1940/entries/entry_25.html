To the vasu Lýipeya of pleasant aspect the kāla Kunala sends good health, much, infinite, and thus I write. You must learn that in Peta-nagara there is corn for paying wages (to the extent of) milima [. .]. Further there is corn for the wages of the guard, milima 3, and corn for his food, milima 1 khi 10. When this Jamavaṃ[na] arrives there, quickly 15 milima of corn is to be given to Jaṃmavaṃna and Ṭhuvaya; no obstruction must be made. They made a decision before Pǵoco, [. .]kto and Pǵita. It is to be given to Kuviñeya as his ration [. . . .] is to be given. If the horse arrives in time [kṣaṇe], thus you must understand.
