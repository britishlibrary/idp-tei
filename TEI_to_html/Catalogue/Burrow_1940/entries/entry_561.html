Or.8211/1435(B)

Section: Obv.
His majesty, etc. . . Sunaṃda reports that concerning a theft, a dispute was judged by the kitsayitsa Luṭhu and the cozbo Kaṃci. An aṃklatsa (camel), a putǵetsa (camel), and an odara (camel) were to betaken from the slave of Jimoya. A written document was made. The putǵetsa and odara (camels) were kept back there. When this sealed wedge-tablet reaches you, forthwith careful inquiry is to be made there. Just as the dispute was judged by the kitsayitsa Luṭhu and the cozbo Kaṃci, in that manner a decision is to be made according to law. If there's any other dispute, they are to be sent to the king's court.
