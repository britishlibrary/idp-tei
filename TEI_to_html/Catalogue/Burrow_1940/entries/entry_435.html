His maj esty, etc. . . . Bhimas̱ena informs us that they employed a camel belonging to him to carry a load of kuv́ana (-corn). It died on the way and did not bring its burden here. When this wedge and seal reach you, immediately careful inquiry is to be made. The traditional law of the land is that of whomsoever a man or beast dies while in state employment, it has to be reckoned (= paid out) from the administration. So you must take this law of the land into consideration.

Note: (3) For ima read ema.
