Section: Obv.
Things deposited by me: 3 gumoca, 1 puc̄h́ama, 4 pasaṃvaṃta made of cloth, 1 kayavatra, 1 [. . . . va]ca, a bow and arrows, 4 vaṣe, 2 krataǵa, pothi, on one occasion 8, 1 rug (koj́ava), 1 carpet, 1 felt-garment, 5 ropes, 4 kaśpiya.

Note: Most of the objects mentioned in this list are unknown. For pasaṃnaṃmo read pasaṃvaṃta and compare paṃzavaṃta in 316, which is obviously the same word. Pothi occurs again in 17 in the phrase cam̄a pothi. It may be the same word as Hindi, etc. pothi < Pr. potthiā, Pa. potthakam, lw. < Phl. postak, but we should expect -st- to be preserved in this dialect, giving a form *posti.
