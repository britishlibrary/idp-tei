Section: 1
E.G.019.x (IOL Tib M 672), aa (IOL Tib M 675), bb (IOL Tib M 676); parts of foll. 3 from one or more copies of a collectaneous work: ll. 10 recto + 10 verso of ordinary dbu can xylograph, rather large size; border lines; marginal number, (fol. 42), Tibetan and Chinese.
(a) 019.x (IOL Tib M 672), right half.
Apparently a dialogue between Buddha and Vajrapāṇi Vajradhara. Sentences of prophecy of the type - thams cad mkhyen pa nyid dang byang chub kyi sems kyang rab tu brjed pa med par 'gyur ro/
recto, 10 bcom ldan 'das kyi ye shes.....shwa ra ma hA bA tsa sarba dharmA ga ga nA ma ....
(b) 019.aa (IOL Tib M 675), fragmentary at l. and r.
(1) Part of colophon of a work (Don shes pa 'od, Artha-jñānāloka?) handed down in a dream in the 'Um bu glang mkhar or 'Om bu gla sgang palace in the time of the Btsan poLha tho tho ri snyan shal (or ¼gnyan btsan).
dam pa'i chos 'byung ba'i snga ltas su lha tho...shal [gyi ]sku ring la pho brang 'um bu ...babs mi rabs ldan 'di'i don shes pa 'od zhes rmi lam du lung bstan te chos ...
For a similar story concerning Lha tho tho ri gnyan btsan see the Rgyal rabs gsal ba'i me long, ap. Franke, Antiquities of Indian Tibet, II, pp. 80-1.
(2) Beginning (recto, 2) of - 'Phags pa Glang ru lung bstan pa zhes bya ba theg pa chen po'i mdo (Ārya-Gośṛṇga-vyākaraṇa).
The well known account of the origin of the Khotan Buddhism (Beckh_1914: p. 70. Mdo 32, foll. 266 sqq.; Stein_1907: 58-, Thomas_1935: I, pp. 1-).
(c) 019.bb (IOL Tib M 676), fragment of left portion: marginal number, recto Brgyad skyobs (zhe drug gong) (also Chinese), verso similarly '46b'.
From a work apparently entitled Brgyad skyobs, 'The Eight Refuges' composed in the time of the Dalai Lama Dge 'dun 'grub pa dpal bzang po (1391-1478 A.D.).
verso, 8, sAkya'i dge slong dge 'dun grub pa dpal bzang pos yun ring [du]... relating to protection from various faults and fears.
