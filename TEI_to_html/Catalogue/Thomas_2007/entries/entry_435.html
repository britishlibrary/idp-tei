A fragment: ll. 8 recto + 8 verso of neat dbu med script, with l. 1 recto in a minute hand, and one or two interlineations.
From a Buddhist story mentioning a king 'Mahapala' and a Brāhman woman with five sons or brothers, who have a conversation with him.
recto, 7-8 de nas braM ze mo 9 po' rgyal po'i pho brang gi sgo ...r bsdad ...spun lnga skyo bsangs la byon pas...
'Then the Brāhman woman sat at the door of the king's palace, having come for cure of the five brothers' sorrow...' (or 'her sorrow for the five brothers')
