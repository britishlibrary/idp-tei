Section: 1
E.G.019.y (IOL Tib M 673), mm (IOL Tib M 682), 024.e (IOL Tib M 691), d (IOL Tib M 692); parts of foll. 4 from one or more copies of a collectionous work: ll. 8 recto + 8 verso of dbu can xylograph, rather pointed, rather large size; border lines; marginal numbers recto and verso in Tibetan and Chinese.
(a) 019.y (IOL Tib M 673), left half; marginal number, recto ka brgya nyi su gong, 'Ka, 120a', verso similarly 'Ka, 120b', also Chinese.
From a ritual text, mantras invoking various Buddhist Tāntrik divinities Vaiśravana recurring. One of the mantras seems to be named (verso, 3).
[Bcom] ldan 'das kyi spyan sngas yid bzhin gyi nor bu (Buddhasya-antike-Cintāmaṇi).
(b) E.G.019.mm (IOL Tib M 682), right half.
From a work in verse, dealing apparently with the duties and prosperity of kings and people.
(c) 024.e (IOL Tib M 691), nearly complete: marginal numbers, recto ka bzhi gong, 'Ka 4a', verso similarly '4b', also Chinese.
From a Buddhist sūtra, Buddha and Ānanda interlocutours. The scene is apparently the Śālavana, (recto, 3) of which Buddha foretells the future sanctity.
(d) 024.d (IOL Tib M 692), marginal numbers, recto ka brgya chon lnga gong, 'Ka, 175a', verso similarly '175b', also Chinese.
From a Buddhist sūtra: description of a paradise. Buddha addresses (verso, 7) a Rigs kyi lha mo (Kula-devatā) Yang dag par sdus pa. A BuddhaRab tu ngang pa'i zhal 'Utpala'i dri brtsags seems to be mentioned (verso, 4).
