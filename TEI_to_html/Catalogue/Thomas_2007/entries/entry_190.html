A left-hand fragment: ll. 6 recto + 6 verso of ordinary dbu can xylograph, smallish size; border lines; marginal title and number lhan [tha]bs, pakyu (for brgya?), '92'.
Lhan thabs.
From a- Bdud rtsi snying po yan lag brgyad pa gsang ba man ngag yon tan rgyud kyi lhan thabs zug rngu'i tsha gdung sel ba'i kang pA ra... (Amṛta-hṛdaya-aṅga-aṣṭa-guhyopadeśa-guṇa-tantra-sahopāya-śalya-uṣṇa-tāpana-anta-kṛt-kanpāra (kātpūra? = khaḍga)).
This is a chapter on 'removal of the hot pain of piercing wounds' (zug rngu = Sanskrit śalya), belonging to a work named 'Sword cutting the noose of untimely death', (sword = ral gri, Sanskrit khaḍga), (Sanskrit Akāla-mṛtyu-pāśa-cheda-khaḍga), which work is a supplement (lhan thabs, Sanskrit sahopāya,) in India Office copy also called gaṇḍūṣikā) to the medical text Bdud rtsi.....rgyud (Sanskrit Amṛta-hṛdaya...tantra). Concerning the work and other E.G. fragments of it see under E.G.013.d (IOL Tib M 3).
