IOL Tib M 88

Section: 1
Ll. 6 recto + 1 verso of square dbu can script, sub-normal size, + ll. 5 verso (colophon) in a different, smaller hand; marginal number gnyis, '2'.
The colophon speaks (verso, 3-5) of a prayer to Btsong kha pa, which is perhaps the poem contained in the MS. The concluding verse also invokes Btsong kha pa under his name, Blo bzang grags.
recto, 4-5
/mgon po khyod kyi brlabs chen mdzad pa la/
/[b]dag cag bsams pas thag pa'i yi...
/rdze(rje) btsun bla ma dam pa khyad rnams kyis/
/chos sku mkha' la khyen brtsi'i chu 'dzin 'khrig/
From the colophon (verso, 3-4) -
/dmigs med brtsi ba'i gter chen spyan ras gzigs/
/dri med mkhyen ....'jam pa'i dbyangs/
/gangs can mkha' ba'i gtsug rgyan btson kha pa/
blo bzang grags pa'i zhabs la gsol ba 'debs/
gangs can = Tibet
