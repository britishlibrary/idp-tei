l.verso: (SIGNA)

translation:
l.r1: there is a land called Samattai, and in Viriñaupura one tree was born and in one fruit from that tree 108 putrajīva nuts come out. And that is that of the parents of the Lord Buddha Śākyamuni. And
l.r2: that tree and the Udumbara flower and the kesarin lion were at the same time as the Lord Buddha Śākyamuni. And therefore the ganetra made of putrjīva is the foremost ganetra of all the lord buddhas.
l.r3-4: And the vajra-kula (is) jade, the ratna-kula jewel, the padmakula lotus, and the karma-kula rudrākṣa. And that is the five-fold mystery ganetra. The meaning of those jāpas is expounded. Why was it said “a hundred” and “a hundred and 21” and “a hundred and eight”? — That is to be known as the underlying nature of kāya-vāk-citta.
