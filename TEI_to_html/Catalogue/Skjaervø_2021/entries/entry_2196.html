translation:
l.[1]: In compassion there truth ..., ... considers, unconsidered does not remain. That was not difficult, that for a long time, according to truth they gave exalted siddhi(?).
l.[2]: On account of evil acts, which ... vāsanas, give ... that right road! ... Amṛtakuṇḍalin krodha-rāja, Arpahiʾ-krodha, Nīladaṇḍa in turn.
l.[3]: Vidyā ... and the others powerful great ones. The best of (in?) dhāraṇīs, Aśigrīva, the su/sat-puruṣa. The two, Ṣaḍbhuja-cara and Pūrvaka-māla. Icchā bīdaubi the four-faced, beautiful one.
l.[4]: The *five mystery-*giver(s) drāviṇḍą krodha cara kīla kīlä krodha Acala, Ṭakkirāja, Hayagrīva in turn. Mahābala, amäsųbāhyį asiddha-karma.
l.[5]: The burner of evil ones, Vajrrāṇḍāsą krodha, the eighty thousand Yogeśvara-krodha Narākṣįʾ, to all of them I bow and do homage. Release me(?)! Give me exalted siddhi!
l.[6]: Restrain the ... in all the directions, the vajra-kośa and the others, the four gate-keepers, the eight pūjaneyas(?), and then the eight mātras(?). Ttārāgyį Ekajaṭila, Trilokya-ttagārä
l.[7]: The things that appear as mirages ... following ... Bhṛkuṭī, Ārya-tārā, as well as Vajrra-śakhalä (Vajra¬śṛṅkhalā?) Ārya-mūmäkä, Sarasvatī, Rayanīḍa, go into (my) body, into my limbs!
l.[8]: Whatever sufferings, illnesses, fears, anxieties, distresses, evil dreams, unfavorable, ... things there are, make all dissolve, like snow in autumn.
l.[9]: dreṣṭrri saṃnāhi [s]i-da saṃttamäkā give me out of compassion this exalted siddhi! If (my?) energy does not ... this siddhi, then the deities of the mahā-bhūtas.
l.[10]: They look after (me/them?) bājąstą: śakrra-kṣeyį-darūṇi together with retinue Mahāketu always. Body ... if they dwell truly(?), the five great powerful amanuṣa-rājas.
l.[11]: As well as the protectors of the directions, the eight gotra-nāgas(?), next all the ones in the five kulas(?). All the deities prrtta of(?) the ones standing in the maṇḍala, all of them altogether as many as there are.
l.[12]: Look at me with your eye of compassion. Let me obtain here in this birth siddhi in the mudrās(?). Recall now this buddha ... as the nirupara(?) bodhi, by the might of its truth.
l.[13]: ... this exalted order of the krodha-rāja. Do not ... as many here in the vajra sphere in this maṇḍala all the lord buddhas altogether who have ...
l.[14]: Stretch out your arms upon me now! Make the karmas dissolve, destroy the covering of vāsana! All the āvaraṇas, severe sins, I have acculmulated in births on account of folly.
l.[15]: Now, you buddha lions of the Law give me confession of all karmas! The ... saṃvara, may it become clean! I confess the karmas. May I not go thither!
l.[16]: In the middle of saṃsāra, in the domain of sufferings, may Śena Tcunä, the king’s life be long, together with the lady queen, the princesses, and the princes. May I remember all births throughout my births.
l.[17]: Making the mudrās succees, they all appear in full view(?). May the success and happiness of a happy time always increase! ... they have destroyed the karmas for me(?), Cā Kīmä Śanä, I who prepared this confession, may I ...
l.[18]: May I see the true pure nature of the Law(?). May I obtain the foremost vajra-sattva together with his assembly. May all the ... appear, all the ..., lokottara. May it all succeed ... quickly for him.
l.[19]: Like Mount Sumeru (and) a seed of mustard. Mount Sumeru I introduce into the middle of the mustard (seed), all of it completely together with the four continents. ... of saṃsāra then I would make turn in the bhāvana.
l.[20]: Then I would introduce all the ... on the tip of a hair, without the saṃskāras I would make jewels(?) ... ...
l.[21]: I who have ... completely that exalted buddha dhyāna(?) vajra-sattva with the four rays Śrī-vajrasattva the confession which Cā Kīmä Śanä has made with his mind is completely finished.
l.[22]: The fifth regnal year of the happy time (regnal period) Tianzun, in the Year of the Sheep (= 971), the month of Ttuṃjārā, the 17th day, ... residing in the palace, ruling in the Law, the bodhisattva Viśaʾ Śūra, king of kings of the Chinese—with a view to ... of his long, divine, exalted, ... life, I Cā Kīmä Śanä prepared, made, and wrote it with (my?) gotra, mind, and senses.
