Two texts starting from either end of the scroll. Kumamoto, 1996. Text a: Chinese-Khotanese phrasebook (Takata, 1988, pp. 203-4). Text b: letter draft. On the back of Chinese Saddharmapuṇḍarīka (Giles no. 2907).

Section: 1
S 5212a: KT III, p. 136 (70)
Chinese expressions written in Brahmi followed by their Khotanese equivalent. Cf. Or.8212/162.

l.1: || śu ttama la: utca vā bara: kī pa tca hvanakhāysa vā pajsa: ttai ma tcīna
l.2: sūraka vā pajsabīka ñau tca lamaṇḍai vā kūśaʾ śā: va ṣī yau-
l.3: va śyai yaiva ra ṣī yīra: hairai kṣamī hairai hūrrū: yauva śa ma na:
l.4: aśtuau vai kṣaimai: caina ttama la: nama vą̄ bara: ttama hvana śī lakhā-
l.5: ysā vā bara: yama ttama lanamva vā bara: ba la: tsa vāpa lai yana
l.6: vā ā :: ī kha che hvana śau tturaka khaysa pajāysa: u ḍa kyaura
l.7: parya tta paryai: sū ttama lattaradya vā bara: bauvanaiśta ma: ana ḍa kha-
l.8: ra: kūṣṭau parī tsvai: śīʾ ba laparya sūraka vā pajsa vā ttraidai: tcīva ttama lamau vā bara
Chinese → Khotanese:

l.1: śu ttama la水擔來 (shuî dān lái) → utca vā bara: “bring water!”
kī pa tca hvana 急作飯 (jí zuò fàn) → khāysa vā pajsa: “make food for me!”
l.2: ttai ma tcīna → sūraka vā pajsa “cook sūraka for me!”
bīka ñau tca la 覔女子來 (mì nyû-zi lái) → maṇḍai vā kūśaʾ śā: “find me a woman!”
va ṣī yauva śyai yai vara ṣī yīra → hairai kṣamī hairai hūrrū: “whatever you wish I shall give”
l.3: yauva śama na要甚麼哪 (yāo shénme ne) → aśtuau vai kṣaimai: “do you wish anything?”
l.4: caina ttama la氈擔來 (zhān dān lái) → nama vą̄ bara: “bring me some felt!”
ttama hvana śī la 擔飯吃來 (dān fàn chī lái) → khāysā vā bara: “bring me some food!”
l.5: yama ttama鹽擔來 (yán dān lái) → namva vā bara: “bring me some salt!”
ba la 邁來 (mài lái) → tsa vā “come here!”
pa lai ya 不來也 or 已 (bù lái yê/yî) → na vā ā :: “he has not come”
l.6: ī kha che hvana一口吃飯 (yī kê? chī fàn) → śau tturaka khaysa pajāysa: “have a mouthful of food!”
u ḍa kyaura 屋里 (wū lî ...) → parya tta paryai: “please come in!”
l.7: sū ttama la蔬擔來 (sū dān lái) → ttaradya vā bara: “bring me some vegetables!”
bauva 無 (wú) → naiśta ma: “there is none here”
a na ḍa khara 阿那里去 (ā nâli qù),→ kūṣṭau parī tsvai: “where does he deign to go?”
l.8: śīʾba la入來 (rù lái) → parya sūraka vā pajsa vā ttraidai: “please enter!”
tcīva ttama la 酒擔來 (jiû dān lái) → mau vā bara “bring me wine!”

S 5212b: KT II, p. 130 (72)
Beneath the text: @ bod-skad “(in) Tibetan”; further below, a Tib. text. Cf. IOL Khot S. 40 lines 13-19.

transliteration:
l.1: aga ṣṭau brrau jsa īnīda kaṇa ma bīysajīda ttī jsā ṣadyai valakyai brrai
l.2: pvaisa kaṇai dvīda : chaugalakai aga a ñū caistaka jīyaka khvai ṣa pā ma kaiṇa
l.3: dvīda vīnai na hamai : brrī va ttā tsvai ttāṣṭa ṣacū kīthāṣṭa gīhyara vā cai-
l.4: ga ttī jsā hva{tta}na khva vā byaihai pada : kamacū kīthāṣṭa ama jsā ttyai
l.5: gīsta jsa maista baiysūśta bvīryau : O || pharāka ya hvaṇḍa ttakyai
l.6: janavai vīra na ṣava basta aysamū habāḍa śā ṣavai aysmū va tvī bai-
l.7: da basta valakā paināṃ caṣṭā : vā ba brrīyā tta ysaiya śuḍa.
l.8: śuḍa ttaurai jsā dūkhū nau pāra gaula hvīra:

translation:
(1) They make (his?) limbs ... let them not seize the stick! Then ask the *faithful little ..., (2) they will beat him (with) the stick. Chaugalakai’s(?) limbs ... so they do not afterward (3) beat him (with) the stick (and) and there will be no pain for him.
(Your) dear one(?) went to you to the city of Shazhou. Help us, (4) O Chinese as well as Khotanese! (?) When he finds a way here to Ganzhou, for you, by that (5) help, you will obtain great bodhi.
There were many men. For a while(?) (6) in the world(?) at night (my) filled mind (was) not bound (= fixed?). One night I fixed my mind upon you (?). (7) ...
