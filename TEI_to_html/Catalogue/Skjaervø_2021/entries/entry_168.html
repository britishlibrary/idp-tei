translation:
(a1) Year 17, month of Mūdracaja, thereof 11 days.
(2) At that time, in the year of the ṣau Vidyadatta here in the village of Birgaṃdara there was this (3) case, that there is a man (called) Puñadatta. He said thus: “I have (4) a son Haryāsaka by name. I am giving this son in *adoption to the wife (5) of my *half brother *Aśnadāysa for this reason that *we have no (6) (other) refuge, (seeing) that, when I, (once) born (and) alive, die, who will even raise (him?).”
(7) So Aśnadāysa took Puñadatta’s son Haryāsaka (8) in adoption in the presence of the haṃdasta, the spāta (9) Salbī, [note a: Cf. Or.8212/1720.] and the parramai *Aśauka, the parramai Dattaka, the parramai (b1) Cegadatta, the parramai Saṃgabuda.
And before those men (2) he paid the prahänaja in full, (3) exactly as much as the parents requested: 500 mūrās and (4) 3 chās of white silk (worth) 200 mūrās.
From the day of today Haryāsaka (5) is the son of that Aśnadāysa. Nobody else has any legal claim there. (6) Whoever may change this case from (what has been) prepared and construed, to the court (7) he will pay 200 mūrās and receive 50 strokes of the stick. And he shall give as prahänaja (8) one mūrā (and) 5 (?). [note a: Cf. D. iii.12 (IOL Khot 3/1) lines 7-8.]
And this deed of adoption shall become authoritative when the parramais (9) put their seals on it.
I, the scribe Bihera, wrote it from the word of Puñadatta. Haryāsaka 3 years.
