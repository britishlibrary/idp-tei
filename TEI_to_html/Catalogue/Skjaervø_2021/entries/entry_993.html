translation:
l.r1: ... are ... Then he should think thus: (Just) now blue rays came out of my uṣṇīṣa supported on the forhead of all beings,
l.r2: and all beings were taken by me for consecration toward the bodhi of the Buddha, and all beings have become buddhas. Just so should he consider all beings through the form of the buddhas.
l.r3: And just as he has thought his own self, just so he should always sit concentrating with that memory with collected mind.
l.r4: Thus is that memory: Best of all upāya-kauśalya, buddhānusmṛti, yoga-mukha, the new bodhisatva by whom
l.r5: I construct all the bodhisatva-caryās without remainder. Because after all all things are the size of the mind (citta-mātra). As many beings suffer turmoil through the turmoil of the mind,
l.v1: they also are purified by the purity of their own mind. And it has been said: The bodhi of the buddhas—there is none it is far from and none it is
l.v2: close to. He whose mind is in turmoil it is far from, he whose mind is purified it is close to. And, moreover, as it has been said in the Dharmagīti-sūtra: The bodhi of the buddhas can not be given by anybody, nor can anybody seize it. Only from the purity of the nature (prakṛti) of one’s own mind
l.v3: (comes) the bodhi of the buddhas. And that is what is called “buddha.” For the same reason that all things (come) from one’s own mind, for the same reason this memory
l.v5-5a: should be performed. For that reason that memory should be performed thus. For that reason now (is) this memory. So many exalted, meritorious, propitious tathāgatas *shall purify the molasses oil (?).
