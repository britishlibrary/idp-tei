translation:
l.r1: However many kriyās there are, those(?) the śrāvakas, according to yāna, count (as) emptiness. And here, according to the Vajrayāna, it is not empty; it contains vargas. And just thus there is the teaching that: On account of what is the ganetra? Toward what is the ganetra?
l.r2: In what direction is ganetra? For the sake of what is the ganetra? On account of what is the ganetra? — Whatever samādāna deity he takes (obtains?), on account the recollection of the heart and essence of that one (is) the ganetra. Toward what is the ganetra? —
l.r3-3a: Toward Śrī Vajrasattva is the ganetra. In what direction is ganetra? — *Rejoicing toward ...-ing for the sake of recognition and worshiping the lotus of the mystery of the deity in the mystery of Samantabhadra (and for) producing buddhas. For the sake of what is the ganetra? —
l.r4: For the sake of the succeeding of the siddhi of the mystery of kāya-vāk-citta is the ganetra. Now, which (is) thus the reverend teacher (who) expounded the ganetra? And from what tantra did he draw it? And toward which yāna? And for the sake of what did he expound it to us?
l.v1-1a: And when(?) will he go to the meaning of the *dwelling on high? As for the body of the reverend teacher who expounds it, he was born in the southern region, western samyasa(?) in China. He dwelt in Labhasalāspāñä and Ttattīśanä, and he was born in Ttattīśanä in Khamasa. His name was Baśrīmajsu. As for the deity who helped (his) siddhi, (her) name was Brmäysattī. In India
l.v2-2a: he learnt the Law. And he overcame the internal and external mysteries, the entire Law. And there he obtained siddhi so great that he *raised (upasthāpaya?) the four nāga-pālas and ten guardians of the directions and constructed workers similar to them(?). And (he achieved) walking in the air and moving in(?) the earth
l.v3-3a: , and he would go in the great ocean like a fish. Equipped with all those good qualities, having obtained the siddi of (his) vipāka, the reverend teacher expounded (the ganetra). — Because upon the teacher (was) the antaravāsa-mahākāśa-guhya-cakra. (?)
l.v4-4a: And from what tantra did he draw it? — He drew it (from?) the *abhyanantara-tantra. And for the sake of what did he expound it? — To present and future yogins, like Śrī Vajrasattva, with a view to the succeeding of the väkṣäʾmas of ...-ing and for the sake of not forgetting the mantra and the deity of the mudrā-samād(h)āna(?), for instance(?), if he were to place *mudrakās on her(?) the yāna ... the four kings ...
