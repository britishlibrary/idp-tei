Or.6393.1: KT V, p. 1 (2); SD II/XXV; SD, TV, p. 52

Section: 1
Report from Vaśäʾrapāña and Arrjāṃ to the spāta Gaudaka. The spāta Sīḍaka, Vaśaʾrapuña, and Gaudaka are mentioned together in Or.6396/2 from (Viśaʾ Vāhaṃ’s) 19th year = 781. Hoernle, 1901, p. 37. PLATE 5c.

transliteration:
l.1: @ aysi vaśäʾrapą̄ñä u arrjāṃ spāta gaudakä śaṃdā haṃbujsai haṣḍi yani
l.2: a ttanvī vī pūri paśāteṃ paṃ-ysārä mūri dvī ysāri mūri ttä dāśeṃ byehi
l.3: u drrai ysāri ttä ni dāśeṃ byehi kh[u] ttą̄ vaña spāta sīḍaki hīśtä ttä mūrä
l.4: ttyena paphūji na-ṃ haḍä hā haur[a] kuṣṭa-ṃ spāta jī ā c- -ye vīra-ṃ haʾci
l.5: nāsāte tta ttanvī gvaṣcą̄ñä himāri mūri haura pīḍakä tta x biysaṃji
l.6: u mūri ni haure ttī cäkvakä spātana paphūji ttī ra prąmāṃ himä khu hā [x]
l.7: haṃguṣṭi viśtä —

translation:
(1) I, Vaśäʾrapuña, and Arrjāṃ embracing the earth (before) the spāta Gaudaka make a report:
(2) I sent (my) son to Ttanvī [note a: B. takes this to be a noun meaning “hostage” or “pledge.”] for 5,000 mūrās. 2,000 mūrās—those I have received in full, (3) and 3,000 thousand—those I have not received in full. When now the spāta Sīḍaka comes to you, those mūrās (4) you collect from him (Ttanvī), (but) don’t give them (yet) to (Sīḍaka). Where the spāta Jī has ... them(?) ... he will take whatever I have(?). (5) Thus Ttanvī is to hand them over, [note b: B.: “distribute,” but contexts seem to indicate “hand over, get rid of,” in accordance with the Buddh. terminology, where gvaṣṭ- is “separation”; SI P 103.43 has puṣa gvaścarä pāra-vaysdāṃ, which Emmerick translates as “Hand out (receipts for) the quittance of the debts.”] (and then you) give (pay) the mūrās (to Sīḍaka).
Thus (first) you obtain the writ (from him) (6) and do not give (him) the mūrās. Then you shall collect the boy [note c: See Studies III, p. 56.] from the spāta.
And it will take effect when (7) he places (his) finger on it. — (8) Arrjāṃ’s finger.
