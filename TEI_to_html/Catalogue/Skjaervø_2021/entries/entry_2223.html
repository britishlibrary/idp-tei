MKhot., neat, archaic ductus. Wooden tablet, broken lengthwise between lines 3 and 4 (a-side); also cracked lengthwise. 15x4.5 cm. InAs., p. 1027.

transliteration:
l.a1: samagṛ saṃgapālānajä biʾsaṃgä śaṃdā haṃbujsai haṣḍä yani —
l.a2: aysä miḍi ḡyastä ka ttara āśiryāṃ hva hva ttaraṃdara drūnai ṣṭāte u aysmya
l.a3: tsāṣṭā aysä saṃduṣṭūṃ. vaña āṃ ttā ṣiṇau yani haspisca hasta padī byi-
l.a4: haṃjarau u kṣīrä byāta yanīrau cu ma kṣīra vaśna hira haṃjsiṣṭa ṣṭāṃde khu
l.a5: vyachīdä u muhu tta pātcä hasta padī byāta yanīrau u p-a t-ā l ra [note a: B. prrattilma.]

l.b1: p- sa x x [x x] pusūṃ x x x x -ura x x -u x vyachīdä u khu mara
l.b2: kṣīra tsāṣṭā himi. u pātcä tta ṣau viṣṇadatta tta byāta yanīrau khu thyau beʾ-
l.b3: mañe jsa mara hīśtä. —

translation:
l.a1: Bowing down to the earth before the assembled Saṅgha in Saṅghapālāna I make a report.
l.a2: I, gracious lord, if there (where you are) the monks’ bodies individually are healthy and
l.a3: there is peace in (their) mind, (then) I am content. Now I am making propitiation to you. Exert (your) effort in better manner,
l.a4: and remember the land. Whatever evil things there are intended here in the land, if
l.a5: they are dispelled and you remember me thereafter in better manner and ...
l.b1: ... I ask ... are dispelled. And if here
l.b2: in the land there comes peace, then afterwards remember Ṣau Viṣṇadatta so that he quickly
l.b3: comes here in blessedness.
