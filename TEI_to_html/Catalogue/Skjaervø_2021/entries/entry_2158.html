translation:
l.1: From the Land on the eighth day they come to Kāśarapä. From the Iron City to
l.2: Kaukvāṃ there are four Chinese towns. For a stretch of three days, the first city
l.3: is called Śaraḍūgä. From it for a stretch of five days there is (a city) called Tcīnä Hauralaka. From it for a stretch of two days there is (a city) called Īchanagarä. There, on a mountain top there are three monasteries.
l.5: From it for a stretch of three days there is a city called Yabhagau at the head of a wide river. From there on the fourth day there is a city called Kakvāhartcai. Below
l.7: a lake is a river. Along that (river) is (another) river (and) a great mountain, and there is that city.
l.8: From there on the fourth day they come into the territory of the Prrūśavas (Burusho). There,
l.9: the head of a golden river comes forth. There is the first city, called Syadiṃ. On a mountain top
l.10: there are three monasteries. From there for a stretch of six days is a city called Baurbura.
l.11: There is a great river called Sīma. There the men enter (the river) on inflated skins. There are four monasteries.
l.12: *Along the river are villages (and) karānas. South of it along the river is a great city
l.13: called Gīḍagīttä (Gilgit), made of stone. There are eight monasteries. The residence of the king is there in four lands(?).
l.14: South of it is the road to India, along that golden river. There on the shore of the river
l.15: is a great city called Śīlathasa. There *along the river are villages (and) karānas on the shore of the river. Again, they enter it with that kind of inflated skin. From Sīḍathasä to Ttīḍī
l.17: there is a stretch of eight days. To the south hither along that river there are walnut trees, jack fruit, balsam pear, (and) deodar (trees). [note a: See Studies I, pp. 50 (ttīta) and pp. 83-84 (banāte).]
There monkeys dwell. In Ttīḍī
l.19: dwells a king called Magalacakra. This is the first Indian city (on the road) to Kaśmīra. One of its *gates is on a mountain.
l.20: To the south, on the shore of the Mahuvi river, south of Ttīḍī hither, (on the road) to India
l.21: for a stretch of two days, there is a great snow-covered mountain called Sargūṇi. One can descend it in one day. Below it in *rocky place is the first town, called Markaṭagrāma.
l.23: There there are three hundred karānas. To the south of it, (after) a stretch of two days, there is a town
l.24: called Varṇavalā. *Throughout that (town) are five hundred large bazaars. To the south of it, (after) a stretch of two days,
l.25: there is a large village called Manattapaurṇa. There is one large bazaar (and) five thousand kapīlaya trees. To the south of it, (after) a stretch of three days, there is a land (city?) called Nalapaṭana. There there is a large bazaar.
l.26: For a stretch of one day there are five thousand kapīlaya trees. That bazaar
l.28: is on the shore of a lake. There are three monasteries with cells of stone (in rock?). *All along that lake there is
l.29: a ... road to Kaśmīra. To the land of Adiṣṭädaṃ is a one-day (stretch). There is the city of Adiṣṭaṃ. That city of Adiṣṭaṃ has three
l.30: entrances. They are from(?) the river called Vīttasa. There there are 20,000 large boats. The western entrance is in the lake of the city. That city is continuous for a one-day (stretch). In all of Kaśmīra there are
l.32: 60,000 great towns. That (particular) one consists of 300,000 karānas, and (each) one karāna of 360 houses. There,
l.33: there is a large monastery with a dharmarāja (stupa) (and) 500 rock cells. Smaller monasteries
l.34: are countless. The king there is called Abimanyagaupta. (There) are many elephants. He has four army leaders.
l.35: The army is 100,000 (and/times?) 100,000, all in armor.
