translation:
l.r1: ... Without destruction they are destroyed. Without the skandhas they appear. 61. Without speaking they have spoken the correct, profound Law.
l.r2: Without going they have gone. Without vijñāna they know. (6)2. Without soul, life, being, without poṣa, person,
l.r3: saṃjñā, without maker, realizer, knower, goer, eater. (63) Without the skandhas there are no skandhas,
l.r4: 18 dhātus, 12 āyatanas. All those are not with them, nor without them. (64) Because
l.r5: in reality all dharmas are equal to all those. And the Buddha is of one kind. (65) Just like a mirage
l.v1: all the world is just saṃjñā, all the buddhas are just saṃjñā, all the fields are just saṃjñā. Through saṃjñā it appears just like this. 66.
l.v2: Because of vijñāna there are forms. Forms themselves are not at all. Vijñāna, O Buddha, is obstructed. Therefore it should be viewed as without form. (6)7. Whatever
l.v3: appears only by saṃjñā and what (are) all the buddhas themselves—there is no difference even the size of a hair. It is just equal to space. 68.
l.v4: Saṃjñā has made this Buddha. By itself nothing is in reality. Thus the three earths: they are only by one’s own saṃjñā. (69)
l.v5: When I construct him (vikalpaya) the Buddha appears, when I don’t he does not appear at all. All these dharmas (are) only on account of one’s own *mind. 70. What ... its nothing but *citta ...
