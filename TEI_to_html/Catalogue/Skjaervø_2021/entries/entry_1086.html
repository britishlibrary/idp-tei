translation:
l.a1: (I?), the spāta Īramaña embracing the earth (before my) wife, say: I ask you after Īrasaṃga and the other children, Jsijsīya and
l.a2: Vilāya, and (our) other kinsmen, with small and big. I am fine here (and) in good health. How could it be otherwise (?) if you are fine
l.a3: (and) in good health, (then) I here consider it an honor to me. About the harvest, take care that you have watered the grain in due time
l.a4: and that you have looked after the mastauña. [note a: B.: tentatively “fodder,” but uncertain.]
Look after the sheep and the other cattle so that you do not lose them.
l.a5: The paʾsīña [note a: paʾsīña- also IOL Khot Wood 33a1.]
pomegranates from Aśnesala(?), give them to ... so that they do not dry out. [note c: B. “perish.”]
Now Īraka from Kaṃdva has gone to you.
l.a6: Take the wheat from him that is for you: six kūsas. And grind the wheat in the house, when the flour has been cleaned.
l.a7: Gather twelve kūsas of flour for me. And if your own pack-animals can go hither, then carry it hither on your own pack-animals,
l.a8: and let just one man drive the animals to me. If they can not go, then let *rented animals carry it here.
l.a9: But only if it is easier for you do it that way. Gather for me three śiṃgas of sesame oil,
l.a10: three ṣaṃgas of dry kūras, a half pārakā of crude sugar, and two thyeba khalavīs. [note d: See Emmerick, 1985b, pp. 302-3, 307.]
And do give him a sāṃśka for me and
l.a11: felt for cloth. I have given *Baṃṣṭa *bows to deliver to you personally. And thus ... for him a good janā, and place it on him/it(?) if that janā does not fit him
l.a12: (or) those who brought (it?). *Let Īrasaṃga come here to me, (though) it is a long way. And there, you just take care of everything on your own!
l.a13: The 16th day of the month Hamārrīja I gave the letter (to be brought) to you.
l.a14: *Month of Hamārrīja.*
l.b: The spāta Īramaña. To be delivered. [note e: Apparently not: “To the spāta Īramaña”?]
