translation:
(1) The spāta Sudārrjuṃ orders thus: [To] the pharṣa [Sāṃdara ...
(2) I have given a writ for the spāta Budarma that: They have ordered (gone off) to buy me a horse. ... (3) were present in [...]. And four of them here were top quality. For them here hemp ... (4) ... a ṣaṃga of fifty, and the hemp amounted to 15 kūsas one ṣaṃga. ... (5) For each man he ordered you 16 ṣaṃgas of hemp.
When you hear the order [quickly deliver] this [hemp]. [note a: Cf. IOL Khot Wood 32.a1 khu parau pvaʾ ttū jsāra.] (6) When you bring me the *debt record [note b: Cf. OIOC Photo 392/57 T.O. 46 (Domoko F 11; cf. KT IV, p. 76: B.: “quittance of debts”); SI P 103.43 pāra-vaysdāṃ, Emmerick “the quittance of the debts.”] of spāta Budarma, when (it is) present here ... (7) there will be no interest. And if they do not request ... for the pack-animals, [note c: Cf. SI P 97.5.1 stūrāṃ va hvaiya-ṃ heḍi.] *new ...
[On ...] the order went out [to you]. SIGN
