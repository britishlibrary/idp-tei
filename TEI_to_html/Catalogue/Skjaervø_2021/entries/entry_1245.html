Fol. 44

transliteration:
l.r2: ... ttū burä hve gyastä baʾysä sīrä hamye āśiʾ
l.r3: rī subhūta avaśiṣṭau āśiʾrya āśiʾ ūvāysā ū(vā)ysye gyasta.
l.r4: dīvitāna aysura-gąndharvāṃ āstaṃna lovya parṣaʾ gyasta baʾysä.
l.v1: haḍi vajrrachedaka ttṛśayä prajñā-pārma samāsye ||
l.v2: siddham vajrrachidakyi hīya ttā diṣṭānta
l.v3: hvañāri: paḍāṃjsyāṃ bāḍāṃ śiña kṣīra śau lakṣa

translation:
l.r2-3: This much the Lord Buddha spoke. They were (all) content: the reverend Subhūti *and the remaining monks, nuns, laymen, laywomen, and the world-encompassing assembly of gods,
l.r4: goddesses, asuras, gandharvas, etc. The Lord Buddha,
l.v1: however, concluded the Vajracchedikā-prajñāpāramitā in three hundred verses.
l.v2: Welfare! The diṣṭāntas of the Vajrachedikā
l.v3: are spoken to you, (those) of former times, in one land, one lakh(?).
