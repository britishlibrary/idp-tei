translation:
l.r1: They establish them in bodhi. As for what is on(?) the mudrā, that is great compassion and the path to bodhi. As for the mudrās there are, that is the underlying nature (prakṛti) of the residing in the triple-tiered world.
l.r2: And the rising up from the ... of the nine yonis. As for the tip of the mudrā, that is the tip of births. And the knot, that is the Body of the Law. And one ... is the reverend teacher of the vajrācārya. As for the
l.r3: ... of the mudrās, that is the 18-fold emptiness, it is the eye of knowledge. As for the teaching of the world of the sattva-dhātu through compassion, as for the Bodhi of the Law and the vajrācārya, those are undistinguished.
l.r4: At the time of *drawing out the mudrās, one should sit down(?), the knots of ... should be released, one should turn (them?). If one were to release them (then), there would be for him crossing of the Body of the Law and the vajrācārya and *breaking of the samaya, and great(?) causing to break of the command.
l.v1: Broken is the samaya(?). The first gotra of the mudrās is shown as follows: henceforth the five-fold ganetra is not suited for the śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, arhats, bodhisattvas. The jewel ganetra and the one of bone and the one from the *kernels of the vargasta trees are not suitable. Thus (it is in the case of) those who do not have mantra, mudrā, (or) samaya.
l.v2: Next, the Vajrayāna has *surpassed the Śrāvaka-yāna and the Pratyekabuddha-yāna in four ways: in view, career, success, and samaya. How has it *surpassed it in view? — It has *surpassed it in the view of Samantabhadra. The career has *surpassed it in prajñā (and)
l.v3: upāya. The success has *surpassed it in the maṇḍala of kāya-vāk-citta, because it has shown the ten-fold gotra of the mudrās. This(?) must henceforth be joined with the gotra.
l.v4: As for the tathāgata-kula (and) the vajra-kula, karma-kula: there the ganetra should be joined according to each kula. That is the middle. There the ganetra is from putrajīva (nuts). jāpa is not suitable together with its pronouncement. ... must be *drawn out. In the southern region of Jambudvīpa in yonder land of India
