translation:
l.[1]: Welfare! [There be] reverence toward the buddha kings, all of them, Āryā-Vairocana and the others, arisen from the gotra of his vajra, born in a jewel.
l.[2]: The holder of the abhiṣeka, born in a lotus, arisen in a lotus, having the nature of truth (tathatā-prakṛti), his name Non-empty, a buddha lion, siddha-vīdyādhara giver of ... by (his) fourfold act.
l.[3]: Those who hear the deliverance in the mystery gone out of the great tantras lead (along?) the six paths of pure pāramitā. They have gone out from the fruition of the bodhi-citta.
l.[4]: The vajra-pāramitā, the buddha mystery, from which the exalted vajra-sattva is named, upholder without remainder of the buddha gotra. Because, without seed there is no vipāka-varga. Through *saṃvara [note a: B.: saṃvṛtya “by connotation.”]
(is) the best foremost bodhi.
l.[5]: On account of the compassion of the four saṃgraha-vastus, by which beings are nurtured toward bodhi, this mahā-pāramitā is called pure, through the four dharmas, this one, which (is) the ratna-vajra.
l.[6]: Gone out from the knowledge of the dharmas in the mysteries, the best foremost jñāna-pāramitā, real, great, pure, *undimmed, *undefiled, what is called dharma-vajra in (this?) manner. 6.
l.[7]: Pure, informed about ādharaṇa(?), true, this karma-vajra which is called a pāramitā, their mother, queen of the four pāramitās, (is) the dāna-pāramitā, which they call Lāsā.
l.[8]: Whatever plesures (there are?), with the help of pure fruition, for the sake of beings in the five gatis, what (is) in the mystery of play and pleasures in turn, by an unawakened gotra are beings *attached(?). 8.
l.[9]: Those garlands *raised up, covered with jewels that *perfume of śīla, *which (are its?) shining members, therefore this name: the deity Mālā the best, the buddha śīla-pāramitā in turn. 9
l.[10]: The kṣānti-pāramitā, this (is) the deity Gītā, this one who makes songs by the tongue of the buddhas. The vīrya-pāramitā, this (is) the deity Nṛtyā, by which they obtain quickly best bodhi.
l.[11]: The dhyāna-pāramitā, this (is) the deity Puṣpā, *unshaken by the ... of desire(?). The best pāramitā of knowledge, this is the deity Dhūpā ... 11.
l.[12]: The exalted meritorious perfumed energy (and) saṃjñā (saṃjñas?), who ... in the forest of saṃsāra, pure vows, she is *undespised by light(?). this (is) the great deity Vajralokā, the best. 12.
l.[13]: She(?) who obtained in the pāramitās best control, good, great, power, pure, true knowledge, this is the great jñāna-pāramitā deity, the repressor (and) destroyer of the flood of ignorance. 13.
l.[14]: Ajitā, this (is) the deity unconquered by the māras; through awakening at the time of awakening, on account of the ākṣepa of the vajra-pāramitā. The divine true command should be set out upon(?). 14.
l.[15]: He knows the self (to be?) pure dharma, anātma, this (is) the vajra-devatā-pāramitā, hidden(?), of those in sequence, the great samādhi(s), the mysteries. He obtains the pure dharma-kāya made of vajra. [note b: See Emmerick, 1981b, p. 451.]

l.[16]: With the help of the entire buddha knowledge, on account of the ripening (paripāka) of skill in the means, she gathers beings from the four-fold *division: therefore my(?) name is the best Vajrāṅkuśī. 16.
l.[17]: On account of the exalted buddha career *overcome, *longing of the senses, true, therefore this (is) best name: Vajra-pāśī, extended within she nurtures the beings toward bodhi. 17.
l.[18]: (Her) gotra *attached to *same-and-otherness she *enters the entrance to the buddha sphere. For the sake of compassion she observes the dharma-dhātu. Therefore this (is her) exalted name: Vajra-sphoṭā.
l.[19]: Whatever ... sarva-dharmas (there are, are) anātma, without beginning, ..., always extingushed: she propounds this Vajrayāna (as) *Mahayana(?). Therefore she is called the exalted Vajra-gaṇṭhā.
l.[20]: The foremost gracious buddha-yāna, to you I do reverence, homage, bending over, with my forehead I do ... worship (and) then all kinds of vāsanas (and) dṛṣṭis ..
l.[21]: Through little care(?) they lead (to?) niravabhāsa(?). May I see the mūlaja buddhas, the deities, the krodhas. At the time of death they appear to him/her as so much(?) tathatā-prakṛti, they give abhiṣeka, just so they appeared exalted to you(?).
l.[22]: They lead from me in their own kulas(?). May I see the vajra-sattva surrounded by his assembly. Filled with rays may I be able to go onto the waves(?) they emit rays (?)
l.[23]: yellow, white, blue, as well as red, green. Those so many rays stand on ... May I have the same share as (and) be equal to the assembly. Without the darkness of folly may I always be purified.
l.[24]: From the directions and subdirections, to unlimited lord buddhas together with the bodhisattvas ... I shall make worship and homage with incense and flowers. I shall go in reverence, I shall always give.
l.[25]: May I obtain the supreme rule of the Law. Whatever *uncounted obstructions I have accumulated in this life, hatever in other lives on account of folly, to these teachers of the same Law(?) may I be able to tell (my) sins(?)
l.[26]: Or whatever evil deeds (I have committed) on account of anger (toward? my) mother and father, through envy ... acquisition of things, calumny and sins on account of desire, or whatever those karmas (are) ... thus for me(?).
l.[27]: Now you, buddha lions of the Law, dissolve (my) karmas, destroy the confusion of the vāsanas. May I turn the formemost wheel of the Law! 16 (or) 36 billions armies of Māra,
l.[28]: all with the vajra may I conquer! May I obtain the four ṛddhi-pādas in turn, the six abhijñas, may I walk in the air, may I cross over ..., may I come to unlimited lokadhātus!
l.[29]: May I very quickly see the *vajra-kula, the mūla-ja cakra-vajra(?), the jewel lotus together with its ... leaf(?). Worship severally of their bījākṣaras.
l.[30]: May I obtain the foremost exalted siddhi, laukika ... lokottara quickly all the beings in the six(!) gatis, standing bent in reverence.
l.[31]: At (my?) invitation may they all enter the bala-praveśa, Abhaya-pura, where all fears are extinguished. May they obtain the great buddha earth! May Śiṃ Kūnä Rau Tcū have a long life!
l.[32]: May he have long life in endless dominion (and) exalted success in his deeds according to his mind! For the lady queen together with the princesses and princes for a long time may success and happiness grow!
l.[33]: Foreign armies, fear and dread, the *disturbance of famine, storm and rain, – ... locusts, ..., as well as illnesses, may they severally not be at all here in our land!
l.[34]: May the mantras and mudrās succeed for Cā Kīma Śani who prepared this confession. – If there had not been for the difficulty of ślokas, ...
l.[35]: I wrote it for the world in the six gatis. May they obtain ... knowledge, ... – May all beings become buddhas, and may also this cakravartin of endless dominion become a buddha!
l.[36]: Welfare. I go in reverence to the vajra-sattva together with his assembly, who has conquered all the sattva-dhātus in the world(?) – two-fold laukika ... command, by support on energy.
l.[37]: They will be seated in the best earth for the sake of the ones to be pitied, very exalted compassionate, for the sake of the ..., – a giver of painful chastisement and punishment in another birth, foremost ..., therefore I go to him in reverence standing bowed in respect.
l.[38]: The buddha-yāna, the best Law, destroyer of woes, the *awaker of wrong knowledge, great earth, – he who ... the hidden mystery of the buddhas of the three times, the great merit earth I am able to fall before the buddhas(?). Through (its) meaning ... he destroys (my?) karmas, mine.
l.[39]: Cā Kīmä Śani’s, whose births are now in this ... land, – (my) senses small, ... in (my) heart it pleases (me) to see the supreme power.
l.[40]: Protect me ... time/land now, so that I may obtain better exalted success, – this exalted community of the vajra-sattva. ... so that ...
l.[41]: They have spoken the *Tantra-kalpa-dhāraṇī, they have placed the mantras, ...
