Section: 1

translation:
(a1) ... you (must) speak ... In the Masters’ mouth there is no word here. Thus it torments my heart. Now, you (must) *say (that) ...
(2) I have made a report [to ...] that my work is difficult. I am getting a būka. [note a: Cf. Hedin 11.3 būki āṃ ni byehī “let me not get a būka!”] You (must) speak to Councilor Mąmi [Śäʾrä] thus: ... (3) If you seek work for me there (where you are), say to Puñarucita how [I/he? must take] care of ...
(4) ... I ask you: Are you ordering me to get it from him [note b: Or read *ttinī “at once”?] or not yet? SIGN

l.b2: śā ye vaña paśā naṃdaki biysīye ʾī āstaṃna kīri makalä parya naṃdaki ni ma -i /
l.b3: u ttī ṣi jsāṃ naṃdaki spaśañañāṣṭi gvāri yuḍe u pyaḍai ṣi hā ṣapą̄ñä x [x x x khu]
l.b4: parau pvaʾ ṣapą̄ñä tti cu vaña hatcaste naṃdaki ṣapą̄ñä ni perri ya ṣapą̄ñä ttye [x x x]
l.b5: peri ʾīṃ tti ṣapą̄ñä cu vā vaña naṃdaki hīsīdi makalä parya SU

translation:
(1) The spāta Sudārrjuṃ orders thus: To the pharṣa Sāṃdara.
Makala had not sent the wine *to him. (2) Now he has sent. Naṃdaka has obtained (some). From today order Makala (to do) the work! Naṃdaka does not ... (3) And then Naṃdaka brought a dispute to the *inspectorate and *obstructed it. He [... went] there to *canteen duty. [note a: See KT IV, p. 100: “kitchen.” Cf. the Tib. byan-ḡyog “cook-service” (Thomas, 1951, p. 252).]
[When] (4) you hear the order, the *canteen duties—those which have now been *canceled.—Naṃdaka ought not to have done *canteen duties. The *canteen duties ought to been done by that [Makala?]. (5) Today, those *canteen duties, when they come [= the turn comes] to Naṃdaka, order them on Makala! COPY
