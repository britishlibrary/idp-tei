translation:
l.1: (1) Reverence to the buddha kings, Āryāvalokiteśvara and all the others, – (2) arisen from that lineage of the vajra, born in a jewel, upholders of the consecration. 1.
l.2: Born in a lotus, arisen in a lotus, having the nature of truth (tathatā-prakṛti), his name Not Empty, a buddha lion, – great example of profit for all the buddhas, as well as the best bodhisattvas, having reached the bhūmis. 2.
l.3: (4) The arhats and hearers, as well as the rhinocerus-horned ones (= pratyekabuddhas) who deign to remain in the sphere of the dharmagarbha. – (5) I have escaped to the .. sphere of the dharmadhātu, full of the nature of truth (tathatā-prakṛti), having the nature of extinction (śamatha-prakṛti), pure. 3.
l.4: (6) Awakened in the sphere of nirvikalpa, clean, gone out from the fruition of the bodhi-citta. – Sāmbhogakāya and nirmāṇa(kāya), exalted, (7) for the sake of all beings in the five gatis,
l.5: They emit their rays of compassion, the elixir of life (amṛta-rasāyana), so that beings may not go to apāyas for a long time, – (8) But they pronounce exalted instructions according to the Law, they take births here as prophesied by the buddhas.
l.6: Having perfect view (samyak-dṛṣṭi), (9) of pure lineage, royal, issued from the family of King Mahā¬saṃmata, – Vaiśramaṇa, the lord Aśoka, King Cayaṃ, the milk-giving (10) lady goddess of the earth,
l.7: the increasing protector, guardian, envoy(?) Avalokiteśvara, exalted bodhisattva, – (11) arisen worthy of the rule over the Jewel Land of Gostanadeśa, to that king of Gostana, the bala-cakravartin-rāja,
l.8: (12) rājeśvara alone ... in all of Jambudvīpa, of the sons of sons(?) of that king heroic, daring, – (13) a king subduer of all kings, the best of rulers over all kings,
l.9: the transformation (nirmāṇa) of Maitreya manifest for a hundred births; (14) to him Śakra, Brahma, and also Nārāyaṇa, Maheśvara, – Skandha, Varuṇa, the king of nāgas, eight bodhisattvas, eight guardians, (15) King Jetasambhava(?), the lady goddess,
l.10: and also the guardians of the (four?) directions, the four guardians of the nāgas, Śukhūṇa, Śakhīma the yakṣas, Dīśamūka, – (16) next Grahavadatta, ... and also the gods and goddesses, the guardian nāgas, gave the kingship, all the marks of the king,
l.11: (17) equipped with the royal members of the cakravartin, and the seven jewels ... they spoke, – (18) they established the kingship, the name of king, victorious in the reign of the vajra,
l.12: Śrī Viśaʾ Dharma, bodhisattva and king, like a lamp (19) the lord Śakra might shine among the Thirty-three gods, among the gods, – like that (he) is ... among the kings of Jambudvīpa, (20) the gracious shizun.
l.13: Of that Viśaʾ Dharma, bodhisattva and king, it was the fifth year, the head (being) (the regnal period) Zhongxing, – and the horse led (21) the year [= 982], the month (was the) seventh, when there arose for him according to his mind, the divine wish,
l.14: the desire for a queen, pure, born among the Chinese, (22) with a view to the continuance of the golden kingly family. – He deigned send envoys hither to Shazhou, a full hundred and fifteen, (23) of heroic roots, best sons of the best,
l.15: all equally meritorious, youthful, worthy of display, ..., virtuous, heroic, (24) meritorious, believing, ..., of (good) family, eminent; – ... of those best ones ..., at all times at peace, Yāṃ the thye-śī, (25) covered with all virtues, born with knowledge and grown in knowledge,
l.16: speaking tasteful and sweet (words), ... in senses, heroic, (26) he saw much in the lands, – having the qualities of a good man, meritorious, Sera the hvūṃ-śī, second in rank of the envoys.
l.17: Famed, heroic, peaceful, energetic, (27) mild, Cā the kama-śī the best of all virtues, – those (three) went as envoys according to the order here to Shazhou.
l.18: Like the land of gods (is) the city of Shazhou, like a lamp, pure (like) gold melted a hundred times, – or like a peak studded with jewels, brilliant, beautiful, like that that land shines together with (its) king.
l.19: ... in this entire Jambudvīpa (36) there are sixteen lands, 86,000 cities, of the lands in this entire Jambudvīpa are inhabitants(?) – with (complete) senses (31) performing good deeds, believing, heroic, knowledgeable, valiant in giving, like in our Golden Land (32) ...
l.20: Then there is in Shazhou the Gūmattīra (monastery), beautiful, quite marvelous displays of buddhas (and) bodhisattvas, – difficult to construct, so beautiful, marvelously made, marvelously constructed. For many belief is quickly born (34) in (their) mind who have seen the dear sight of the buddhas.
l.21: And their sins are destroyed, their commotions are calmed, (35) the acts smeared with kleśas, all those are destroyed for them. – Among those envoys folly and ignorance, like the śala tree ...
l.22: (36) ... when a hare steps there ...
