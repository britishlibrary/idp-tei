translation:
l.110: We make a report to the court.
l.111: The seven princes, we went out in front of the royal sight (saying) “we are going to gūttauṣąnä.”
l.112: The order was for us “do not stay long in Shazhou.” When we gave Capastaka a report for you,
l.113: till now he has not yet directed (us) to Ganzhou. All the animals our men had are lost. Our clothes are lost.
l.114: ... there is for the animals, nor for clothes. A there is no one with whom we can get out (and go to?) Ganzhou.
l.115: And the animals Ttaya-śāṃ had, they were since lost. There is nothing any more for the horses,
l.116: nor any clothes. Neither Chīkä the prramāṃ nor Ḍū the puye has any animals. How (can) we then
l.117: come to Shuofang, since we have neither gift nor letter for the Chinese king? Things for food and drink
l.118: will go ... How can even so much(?) favor extend to us? Till now there in Ganzhou many
l.119-120: men have died. We have no food. How were then an order to come? How can we have to enter a fire (from which) we can not bring ourselves back!
