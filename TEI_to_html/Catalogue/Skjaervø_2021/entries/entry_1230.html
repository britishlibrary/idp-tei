Fol. 1r contains, apparently, the colophon of a preceding text and an introduction to the Vajracchedikā. It has a Chinese stamp. There are three different hands. The original(?) colophon is written in bold script in three lines: the single word āśiʾrī at the top edge to the right (line 0) and two lines (1-2), followed by the intro¬duction to the Vajracchedikā (line 3). The lower left corner has writing apparently in the same hand (line 4). At the top of the folio are two lines (0a-b) continued between lines 1 and 2 (1a) and below (the akṣara ska) and to the right of line 2 (2a-b) containing a note regarding a legal dispute. At the bottom edge of the folio (2c) is a triśaraṇa. Hoernle, MR., pls. v-xi; fols. 1r/v and 33r in Ser., pl. CXLIX. The Vajracchedikā begins on fol. 1v, decorated with a yellow buddha and flowery decorations. PLATE 7a.
