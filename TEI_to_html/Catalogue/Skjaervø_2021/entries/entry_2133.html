translation:
l.1-2: ... with ... a ground bean should be soaked. (Such) a pill destroys red swelling. Castoroil and sesame should be placed in equal quantities
l.3: and finely ground. It should be made into a pill with camel’s fat. Afterward
l.4: it should be dried. Perfume of bdellium and myrobalan should be placed in equal quantities and ground
l.5: and blended into it. It should be soaked with cow’s urine. The pill should be heated and
l.6: placed on the lower abdomen. It removes enlargement of the abdomen and swelling. bahauʾya, bauṇva, ṣavara
l.7-8: should be placed in equal quantities and pounded together. It should be bound on the swelling. It removes red swelling. vmīrāṃ, aysāʾya, bird cherry(?), dry radishes, and ground flour
l.9: should all be placed in equal quantities and finely ground. It should be cooked with *wine. yamaka oil
l.10: should be mixed in. Rock salt should be sprinkled on it. It should be bound on the gut. It removes red swelling
l.11: and constipation of urine. ... grapes and dry radishes should be placed in equal quantities
l.12: and pounded. Flour should be mixed into it. It should be cooked with *wine and bound on the gut. (It makes)
l.13: a pill against swelling. Bird cherry(?), dry radishes, bladder sorrel marrow, ground flour, bauṇva,
l.14: ṣavara, rājānamva to be burnt(?), pigeon dung, sesame, and linseed
l.15: should all be placed in equal quantities finely ground. It should be cooked with *wine and mixed. Sweet flag and orrisroot should be sprinkled on it.
l.16: This pill, which is called daśāṃga, should be bound on the gut. It removes red swelling. Dry radishes,
l.17: castor oil plant, mujsāka, and the dregs of *wine should be placed in equal quantities and ground. It should be cooked with *wine
l.18: and mixed with old ghee. Costus should be sprinkled on it. It should be bound on the gut.
l.19: It removes red swelling stomach pains. When the feet are swollen, this is the pill:
l.20: the linseed in wheat(?) should be heated and ground. ... should be made, and a pill should be made
l.21: with the dregs of *wine. It should be mixed with old cow’s urine. It should be bound on the feet.
l.22: It removes the swelling. Leaves of castor oil plant should be soaked
l.23: and mixed with sesame oil. Rock salt should be sprinkled on it. It should be bound on the feet. It removes the swelling.
l.24: Sesame, turneric, liquorice plant, and yellow flowers should be placed in equal quantities and finely ground.
l.25: It should be soaked with bahauya. (Such) a pill destroys swelling of the skin. White onion(?) and white perfume should be placed in equal quantities
l.26-27: and pounded together. It should be kneeded with ghee. (Such) a pill is applied against red, warm swelling. Dry radishes, the dregs of *wine, and castor oil plant should be placed in equal quantities and pounded.
l.28: The pill should be cooked in old oil and *wine. It should be mixed with old ghee and costus should be sprinkled on it.
l.29: It removes the swelling. ... root, ..., red ochre, should be *placed in equal quantities with dried grapes, cow’s oil
l.30: and pounded. It should be soaked in cow’s urine. This pill removes hard swelling of the skin.
l.31-33: ..., mudar, sesame, dry grapes, and fat should be placed in equal quantities and finely pounded. It should be kneeded with cow’s oil. This pill is a remover of swelling of wounds. These are the pills applied against liver (swelling). Goat’s brains should be kneeded together, sugar should be sprinkled on it,
l.34: and hīśaʾ. It should be bound on the liver (and the ailment) is removed. Goat’s brains
l.35: should be mixed together, and wrapped on to the skin. (Such) a pill is applied against liver (swelling). Sesame, linseed,
l.36: green beans, and rice should be placed in equal quantities and pounded. This pill should be cooked with milk.
l.37: Sugar should be sprinkled on it. It removes the swelling and disturbance of the breath. Castor oil plant, tharka,
l.38: marrow, ... grapes, sesame, pomegranate seed, camel’s fat, mudar, goat’s
l.39-40: fat should be placed in equal quantities and finely pounded. The pill should be mixed and bound on the liver. It removes red swelling and cough. Grapes, sesame, red sandal, and rice
l.41: should be placed in equal quantities and finely pounded. It should be cooked with milk. (Bound) on the liver (such) a pill is the remover of aches.
