translation:
l.a1: “... ghee and sesame honey and sugar and rice and the juice of salt (brine?) and [the ...] of wild animals [...]
l.a2: become heretical. So many benefits arise in the world. And now in that manner [one refrains] from heresy. ...
l.a3: ... who themselves have refrained from heresy and who have held others back from heresy. ..
l.a4: have produced great benefits for them. But hear now this! And there then [the Buddha], Lord of Lords, called [Śāriputra]
l.a5: and spoke to him thus: Śāriputra, the beings who are heretical become ungrateful toward me [and toward the Law and]
l.a6: toward the saṅgha and toward (their) parents and toward all well-doing beings, because the three ...
l.b1: they polish off evil deeds and thus perform great, heavy sins, he polishes off the saddharma-prakṣepa-karman (= the sin arising from the rejection of the true Law) and so on
l.b2: (thinking): Did not the Lord Buddha say: The differences (between) either the śrāvakayāna or the pratyekabuddhayāna ... the *Mahayana ...
l.b3: [However many] sinful acts there may be, there is no sinful deed greater than just this saddharma-prakṣepa-karman. [On account?] of this ...
l.b4: ... there may be, and woes do not please him. *Therefore I shall be grateful toward the three jewels and [toward] (my) parents ...
l.b5: ... he should seek good kalyāṇamitras, who expound to him the right Law of the Buddha, how false views do not ...
l.b6: O Śāriputra, great bodhi-seeker, many beings have performed right views. ...
