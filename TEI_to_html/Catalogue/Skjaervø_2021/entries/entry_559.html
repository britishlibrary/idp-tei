translation:
l.a1: Cloths belonging to the *Sogdian Krrapūśira from a certain *Surade. In this account, thirteen chās are (still) owed of them/to me(?).
l.a2: 10 cloths belonging to Syą̄nāṃ’s Videsā.
l.a3: 23 cloths belonging to Si Vidatta son of Sargīsa.
l.a4: 16 cloths belonging to Nuṃdārrjuṃ of Pajūma(?). The biśta’s(?) cloths became [...].
l.b1: Among the ...-s of Pajūma, Nuṃdārrjuṃ the biśta’s(?) men (are) 7. So if (it was) 53 mūrās per man, his mūrās became 371 [= 53x7].
l.b2: ... (has) 3 army [note a: Cf. Hedin 42, a list of army persons owing (missing) in various towns.]
men. His cloths became *53. The working men (were) 13. For each man 2. His cloths became [26].
l.b3: ... the biśta’s(?) cloths became 19.
l.b4: ... *Ḍī Kvāṃ (Ling Gong?) (had) 12 men. So if (it was) 52 mūrās per man, his mūrās became 636 [= 13x52!].
l.b5: ... *army men (were) 4. The cloths were — chās. The working men, their cloths were [...].



(OS)
Kha. i.13, numbered consecutively by Hoernle(?) as fol. 1 to fol. 15. The IOL plate labels have Kha. i.13/1, Kha. i.13/2, etc., with the superscript indicating the sequence of plates, but there are no superscripts on the ms.
