translation:
(1) This document the pharṣa Bara signed and Braṃgala.
(2) This document (was written) for this reason that (3) Yagura has received water *to make money* (4) from the pharṣa Bara and Braṃgala (5) for 2500 mūrās. From the pool of Phaṃnai, (6) one day one night the water from the dammed-up river—two (7) parts water the pharṣa Bara leads off and one part Yagura.
(8) When the pharṣa Bara pays his mūrās or the people of Phaṃnai, [note a: phaṃnāja- may be the adjective of Phęma, i.e., “from Phęma,” rather than “from Phaṃnai.”] (then) Yagura, (9) at the beginning of the year, gets 2,500 mūrās, only he does not receive *yearly interest*. (10) And (if) another man shall in turn buy *stray water (from him), (11) (then) he (only) pays a couple of mūrās.
These were the witnesses there: the pharṣa Chuṃgula (12) and Ṣṣanīra son of Malārrjuṃ(?) and Sucaṃdra.

translation:
(b1) Year X days 27 of the month. This (is) the regnal year of the gracious lord king of kings of Khotan (2) Viśaʾ Dharma.
At that time in the second year of the ṣau Hviṃdū, here (3) in the village (of) Birgaṃdara there was this case, that the pharṣa Bara and Braṃgala said: “We have (4) our water from the pool of the people of Phaṃnai, [note a: Cf. Or.11344/9.] one day and one night from the dammed-up (5) river. This we now put up (for sale/lease) as money-maker (for the buyer?), 2,500 mūrās for a third share.”
So Yagura received (6) this water as money-maker (for him?)—(paying) with his own mūrās—together with (any) profit-maker (to lead off) to *his own *property. (7) So Yagura paid those 2,500 mūrās. We Braṃgala and Saṃgaśūrai and Puñadarma received those 2,500 mūrās in full.
In the presence of the pharṣa Bara and before (c1) the relatives they construed the case thus:
They shall work together in the same field (2) (in order) to send (grain) into storage. The pharṣa Bara receives 2 kūsas grain from the store and Yagura receives one.
(3) If the villagers of Phaṃnai buy up the water, then Yagura receives his 2,500 mūrās.
(4) And if afterward the pharṣa Bara has his own mūrās and Yagura (5) receives his 2,500 mūrās, only he would not receive *yearly interest* from them. The pharṣa (6) Bara, however, does not receive Yagura’s mūrās without the people of Phaṃnai.
This case had come into the *assembly before the pharṣa (7) Chuṃgula and the pharṣa Vikrāntadatta.
The following were the witnesses: Ṣanīra, Saṃgadatta, (8) Sucaṃdra, Īrasaṃga, Puñaudaka, Suvidatta of Paʾ, Buttąnaka of Phaṃnai.
(9) And this document shall become authoritative when the pharṣa Bara signs it.
