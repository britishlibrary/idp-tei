(1) ... Whoever [makes] this polished and settled case invalid ... (2) ... [as] agrees with the place. [note a: Cf. Urumqi 1: (r8-v1) cu ttū gvārä śūʾstä padeṃdā -au x na hamīhyī ā-[t]-ī vā aprąmā yanāte saṃ x tta tta daṃḍä braśtä khu kṣīrū dāna samī “Whoever should change this case which has been *prepared and executed* ... or makes it invalid, only [...] thus the stick will fall as is according to the law of the land” (see Skjærvø, 2002b).]
And I wrote it ... (3) from Kha [note b: Cf. kaʾra si kha ysanaji “the scribe Si Kha from Ysana” (Or.11252/16).] Yausa’s word.
And this letter [shall take] effect (4) [when X] places (his) finger [on it].
