translation:
l.r1-v4: [... said, “How, O gracious Lord] Buddha, should a bodhi-seeking bodhisattva seek a kalyāṇamitra?” The Buddha said to him: “If, son of the house (kulaputra), a bodhi-seeking bodhisattva always wishes to see kalyāṇamitras and he performs the pāramitā of energy (vīryapāramitā) for the sake of (obtaining) kalyāṇamitras, then he should walk 100 leagues (yojana) on burning fire to where he loves the Law. Equipped with those four dharmas a bodhi-seeking bodhisattva should seek a kalyāṇamitra.” Then the bodhisattva Vajrapāṇi spoke thus to the Buddha: “How, gracious Lord Buddha, should a bodhisattva remain in a forest retreat (āraṇyāyatana) where he is free from the troubles related to a householder (gṛhastha)? And as for the meeting with non-kalyāṇamitras, should he therefore leave (his) house and should he leave behind whatever little he has? ...”
