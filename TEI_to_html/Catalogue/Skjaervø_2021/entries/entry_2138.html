translation:
l.1: The imploration, invitation of the great Prince Tcū Syau ...
l.2-3: We take refuge with the lords of lord buddhas in the ten directions, of the three times, standing in full view, all the great bodhisattvas and arhats who have reached the great earth,
l.4: meritorious, well-doing beings. Thus, in the eastern direction, in the Abhīrati
l.5: world sphere, hail to the lord buddha Akṣobhya and all the other
l.6: lords of lord buddhas. In the southern direction, hail to the lord buddha Ratnaketu and
l.7: all the other lords of lord buddhas. In the western direction hail to the lord buddha Amitāyu
l.8: and all the other lords of lord buddhas. In the direction of the Seven Sages
l.9: hail to the lord buddha Dundubhisvara and all the other
l.10: lords of lord buddhas. In the upward direction, hail to the lord buddha Bhadraśrī and
l.11: all the other lords of lord buddhas. In the downward direction, hail to the lord buddha Vairocana
l.12: and all the other lords of lord buddhas,— and whatever lord buddhas there are sitting in the ten directions and subdirections,
l.13: for instance, the lord buddha Dirṇaidąra, the lord buddha Śāntendriya,
l.14: the lord buddha Candraprabha, the lord buddha Narāyaṇa. 1.
l.15: And whatever lords of lord buddhas are here in the middle of the world sphere Sahā, in the temple of the Ratnavyūhamaṇḍala,
l.16-17: in the Ratnarāśi(?), sitting on a bejeweled, thousand-petaled, white lotus, surrounded by 16 trillion bodhisattva kings, hail to the daśa-bala-cakravartin mahākāruṇika(?)
l.18: the teacher Śākyamuni and the other lord buddhas.
l.19-24: And whatever 20 thousand bodhisattva kings there are sitting in the dwelling of the Tuṣitas, mahā-rāja-abhiṣeka-prāpta, led by the bodhisattva Āryā-Maitreya: the bodhisattva Āryā-Maitreya, and the others: the bodhisattva Āryāvalokiteśvara, the bodhisattva Āryā-Mañjuśrī, the bodhisattva Āryā-Kṣitigarbha, the bodhisattva Āryā-Ākāśagarbha, the bodhisattva Āryā-Bhaiṣajyarāja, the bodhisattva Āryā-Samantabhadra, to the bodhisattva Āryā-Vajrapāṇi. And whatever eighty
l.25: hundred(?) śāstanābaibaraprrasāda nāga kings there are, such as Nada,
l.26: Upanada, Grahavidatta, Sūmapuṇya, Hulura, Bujsajsa, Ṣanīraka. 2.
l.27: And whatever very powerful goddesses there are, such as Śakhīmaṃ, Śaukacaumauṇya,
l.28: Hāritī ..., Dṛḍhā Deity of Earth, the Deity of the City, the Deity of Kings,
l.29: to the goddess Deity of the Palace. Finally, the gods protecting the directions. Altogether,
l.30: the protector deities inhabitants of the city of Shazhou, whose
l.31-33: images are here. For the three billions of very powerful lord buddhas in the various dwellings of gods and dwellings of nāgas belonging to the various asssemblies (of monks).
l.34: At the time of the hearing of the Law, wherever you may be, deign to cherish all our invitations!
l.35: Deign bless for us here the great maṇḍala-sthāna in the sections of that place!
l.36-37: The earth covered by golden sand, there deign to bless the individual lions, *wolves, elephants, seats!
l.38: Deign enter the various ... of them all! Deign make worthy through (your) great compassion this their dharma-dāna of the Law!
l.39-40: Whatever may have become here for the great Prince Tcū Syau conveyance(?) into the three fields through (his) accumulation of heavy, extended merits and roots of goodness,
l.41-43: that very foremost worship and acts of worship for the standing lord buddhas of the three times as well as the (acts of worship) such as prabhāsadharma-dhātu-spharaṇa-viśeṣa-samudgata, (that) deign to ripen (for him)! 3. The pāṇi(?)-jñāna-sambhāra of the bodhisattvas,
l.44: the dharma-dāna of the protecting deities may he deign to ripen (for him)!
l.45: He shall deign to find for him(?) by support(?) on their dharma-dāna power, strength, splendor, and growth!
l.46-47: And being powerful and strong he shall deign to produce (samāpad) for them (their?) so many kinds of and ... samādhis.
l.47-49: As many medicines, foods, and drinks the great Prince Tcū Syau may deign to prepare, (all) those ... on the exalted bodies of the bodhisattvas.
l.49-50: The forty-four severe illnesses will in an instant no longer obtain control over him. 4.
l.51-52: Supported on whatever ... abhiprāya he may have deigned perform, may this ... prayoga, this ... quickly go to success.
l.52-54: ... may he have a gati-viśeṣa in the gatis of the buddha ... of King Śrī Vijita Saṃgrāma, a sthāna-viśeṣa, a upatti-viśeṣa!
l.54-55: May he have a meeting with the lord buddhas and the teachers of the Law, and (his) kalyāṇa-mitras!
l.55-57: May there immediately, O Theyi Pa, O great princes and princesses, be one ... of ... fortunes!
l.57-59: May there be, for the great Prince Tcū Syau’s ... and the other ..., for them at all times ... fortunes!
l.59-60: May there be for sickened beings full of illnesses separation and going away from the illnesses.
l.60-61:
l.61-62: May he always, go to a gati *unattached (to the kleśas?) in the worship and service of the Three Jewels! ...
