S 2469: KT II, p. 130 (71)

Section: 1
24th regnal year. On the back of Chinese Suvarṇaprabhāsa (Giles no. 2199), Chinese date = 7(?) June 746.

transliteration:
l.1: || 24 mye kṣųṇä aśi salya hamārrījä māśtä 10
l.2: mye haḍai. baṃ mistä tcai-syāṃ pasti ye cva ma baudhasa-
l.3: ttä pasti pīḍe pharāka ma balą̄hä: haura pa-
l.4: sti hauḍe jastāṃ baʾysāṃ pyaṃtsä ą̄na karmadīśeʾ-
l.5: ni ttaveṃ va ṣīka yaṃ khāṃ būkä saṃgakä u kharūṣä
l.6: u paḍai u tta hvaṃnä saṃgai vī burakye tti hā
l.7: pā puñakuśilämūlä puḍāṃdä nva hvāṣṭä

translation:
(1) Of the 24th regnal year, in the Year of the Horse, of the month of Hamārrīja on the 10th (2) day.
Baṃ the great Tcai-syāṃ had ordered: “What the bodhisattva (3) had ordered me to write here, (is) much ... (4) He ordered gifts to be given. Before the lord buddhas make confessions of sin (5) ... Saṃgaka, Kharūṣa, [note a: Kharūṣa also in IOL Khot 74/3 39v2 and elsewhere, see Kumamoto, 1991b, p. 69.] and Paḍai. And thus all the way to(?) the saṃgha of Khotan. They (7) then promised to deliver puṇya-kuśala-mūlas to them. Accordingly(?), the teacher ...



(OS)
Kha. i.13, numbered consecutively by Hoernle(?) as fol. 1 to fol. 15. The IOL plate labels have Kha. i.13/1, Kha. i.13/2, etc., with the superscript indicating the sequence of plates, but there are no superscripts on the ms.
