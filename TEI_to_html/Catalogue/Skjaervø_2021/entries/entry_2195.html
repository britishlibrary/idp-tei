Hoernle’s “Ch.i. 0021. b.” Vajrayāna texts. Guide2 [18.37]. The manuscript contains three texts: Ch.i.0021b.b (65 lines) covers the whole of one side; Ch.i.0021b.a (47 lines) covers four-fifths of the other side; the third text (13 lines) covers one-fifth of the same side, but starts at the opposite end of the manuscript, so that the ends of the two texts meet (cf. Ser., p. 1452). The third text was not read by Bailey; only individual akṣaras can now be identified, and it is not certain that it is part of Ch.i.0021b.b, though it is possible. Present location unknown. Photo B13451-58 and microfilm. Photos and microfilm are difficult to read; the present text is after Bailey. Cā Kīmä Śanä also wrote the Jātakastava (Ch.00274, IOL Khot 65-82).
