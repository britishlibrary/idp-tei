translation:
l.r1: ... he rode in that direction. When he came to the gate, he saw this pearl necklace. He doubted: “That necklace
l.r2: has become a snake. It begins to long for men.” Then the king became faint. He gathered the medics: “What medicine do [you] know for poison?”
l.r3: He ordered: “That snake must be killed, either by the powers of spells or by the powers of weapons! Either [it must be bound?] in the house
l.r4: or be burnt by fire. Quickly ... That pearl necklace *deceives people, it ... people ... [so that]
l.r5: they can not do ...” Then those medics heard this order of the king’s. Some washed, [some began]
l.r6: to recite, and some purified ... Everywhere ...
l.v1: we shall restrain (it). All men in the land of Śrāvasti were in commotion, so much so that the laywoman Viśākhā [heard it?]
l.v2: As soon as she had heard it, her mind became extremely pleased. Thus she [said]:
l.v3: “It is my necklace, and it is definitely not a snake, because neither water nor fire [... nor]
l.v4: indeed ..., and they do not with their powers make people’s things full of worms, and [they do not] take (them) away.
l.v5: With the intrepitude accumulated through works of ... I shall pronounce this word thus.” Then the laywoman Viśākhā
l.v6: herself went there. She ordered thus to the servant girl: “That is not a snake. That is just my necklace.” ...
