translation:
l.1-2: ... bound to the kingdom. We wish for the eight pleasant commodities [devoid of?] the five ... guṇas with a view to ... of ...
l.3: escaping from the prison of saṃsāra. The śrāvaka-pratyeka-buddha-yāna
l.4: deliverance he went under the rule of birth from the bhavāgra down to the avīcī hell (and)
l.5: pitiful death. For the sake of the benefit and happiness of the three-tiered earth
l.6: equipped with the three bodies.
l.7: We seek the very foremost bodhi of the buddhas. Speak! Do not speak! As many
l.8: upakaraṇas they need for the pure bodhi, such as gold, silver, coins,
l.9-10: pearls, all the jewels and coins will be complete for them. May we not come under the rule of others! Not braṃgaras, not in an inferior family, not lowly, nor may we
l.11: be lacking in aṅgas and pratyaṅgas. Throughout all births, by their hand (= help), may they be jewels
l.12: like the cintāmaṇi jewel. May there be immeasurable wealth for them, immeasurable abandonment,
l.13: immeasurable dākṣiṇeya. May we not come under the rule of greed for those. May we not
l.14: destroy their buddha (bodhi) mind, nor (their) compassion, nor (their) vows. Throughout all births
l.15: may we, like Prince Sudhana, obtain truthful ..., teachers, (and) kalyānamitras.
l.16-17: May we be able to please them the way hail! the bodhisattva Mahāsthānaprāpta, thus the bodhisattva Āryāvalokiteśvara’s compassion, Ākāśagarbha’s power,
l.18: Kṣitigarbha’s career, Samantabhadra’s deliverance, Mañjuśrī’s vows. And
l.19: may we not envy them. May we be able to deliver as many of them quickly across the vaineyas
l.20: as hail! the bodhisattva Bhaiṣajarāja delivered. May we be able to confess as ransom for those beings such
l.21: karmas, āvaraṇas, ..., sins, ... as hail!
l.22: the lord buddha Akṣobhya confessed. May we be able to carry as ransom for those beings such woes and hardships
l.23: as hail! Mahākārṇaka Śākyamuni the Buddha, lord of lords,
l.24: carried in his career. May we quickly
l.25: obtain so much abhiṣeka for us toward bodhi as hail! the bodhisattva Āryamaitreya obtained.
l.26: May *there be for us such a one of their buddhakṣetras like the Sukhāvati lokadhātu. So many merits
l.27: (and) roots of goodness may we bring before hail! the bodhisattva Amitāyu into his safe-keeping, (and)
l.28: may they be remembered at the time of our death. May Samantavairocana extend (his) rays of compassion upon us.
l.29: May the vaineyas be extinguished for us. May the eyes of the Law be purified for us. By his firm support (adhiṣṭhāna)
l.30-31: may we recognize that like a dream all dharmas are empty, all (are) like magic (and) illusion. May they fulfill the pāramitā called upāya-kauśalya (skill in the means of salvation), the ten
l.32: earths, the ten powers. May they fulfill the ... buddha-dharmas. Their wishes fulfilled,
l.33: may all living beings become buddhas in the world. Speak thus: Then
l.35: may we, the donors such-and-such ([34a] the dvaipiṭika Cadrākara-bhadrasīha), become buddhas.
l.36: Reverend ones, protect him! There are the donors such-and-such, the kalyānamitra, reverend, from the beginning.
l.37: May the entire Kāśavitria bhikṣu-saṅgha deign to ripen (their roots of goodness?)
l.38: by (their) pūjā-karmas. They deign to ask (whether) the bodies of the reverend ones each and every one are healthy.
l.39: May it be in (their?) mind. They count happiness, satisfaction, and contentedness at the time (in the land) of ...
l.40: The reverend ones from ... from the bhikṣu-saṅgha assembly. Here
l.41: they obtained the seing of the front(?) of the exalted mahācaitya. They deigned utter to the donor:
l.42: Let them please make for us then in the Three Jewels merits and roots of goodness in extensive manner,
l.43: goodness and dheya-dharmas, even more
l.44-45: in the service of the bhikṣu-saṅgha assembly. For that reason they invited previously the reverend ones of the bhikṣu-saṅgha. With exalted buddha mind the deigned
l.46: extend the invitation. They did not consider ... Very many of them
l.47: count .... Now they deign ever and again to send you invitations.
l.48: They deign to ... the *harsh times. The service and worship are completed. But then
l.49: (you) the reverend ones of the bhikṣu-saṅgha must get away (and go back) to your caitya-vandana. But
l.51: you must then congratulate and ripen so much worship and pūjā-karma
l.50: for the hail! lord buddhas, bodhisattvas, deities, and guardian (deities). You must get away (and go back) to your
l.52: saṅgha-sthāna. And then, each in his own place and on his own seat,
l.53: you must practice restraint. You must enjoy service and worship with the help of the donors:
l.54: sarvopakaraṇas (all utilities of life) by begging. The donors then deign to utter thus:
l.55: Whatever there may have been here (on?) this earth,
l.56: as many merits and roots of goodness as we may have performed earlier in the Three Jewels,
l.57: whatever conveyance there may have been here of those two-fold puṇya-jñāna-sambhāras, the merits and roots of goodness that have been ...-ed.
l.58: Until bodhi has been realized the pure merits and roots of goodness
l.59: they will share with the entire Kāśivitra bhikṣusaṅgha.
l.60: The reverend ... and the others, until sarvanirvāṇa
