translation:
l.1: ... the spāta ... I make a report ...
l.2: The reverend Sureṃdra the kṣī I — O masters(?), raised a son
l.3: from when he was a child till (now). He, for his part, helped him to be initiated in monkhood(?). And there was much ... for me. Two or three times he has ...-ed them here(?).
l.4: He spoke such and such unfitting words to me as do not suit a boy: You have only endured(?), in the land it is an evil time.
l.5: I did not place (him?) for (our) masters the Tibetans(?). Now Misala has gone to you in Birgaṃdara (thinking): I shall do work. If I were to go henceforth, whatever grain I may have obtained,
l.6: all that I would have gathered (and sent) hither. And that son here, the kṣī of Kna, the wife of the reverend Sureṃdra ... But when I came to Birgaṃdara,
l.7: I found (him?) there in the house. Next, the ... which they ate, would have been to be thrown away. Next I threw it away. But over that wife of his there
l.8: arose causes for quarrel from the Tibetans, and they requested from him *five cloths of twenty ... And ... the reverend Vinīyabhavä took the case.
l.9: He made a written statement (as follows): Whatever pretexts may arise, I shall decide it for them. And you just give me as much as three kūsas (of) wheat and three kūsas (of) wine and (of) cloth
l.10: twenty chās. And the reverend Vinīyabhava gave/paid them, the Tibetans, nothing. And the son, with the son the reverend Sudana bought one female donkey(?),
l.11: four thick cloths. The Tibetans canceled three cloths. Now the son has delivered ... six kūsas of grain for the pack animal(s).
