Or.8210/S.1776(A)

Section: V.1
[Li dai fa bao ji]
This is a fragment of 'Li dai fa bao ji' (See Or.8210/S.516/R.1) containing the biographies of the fifth and sixth Patriarchs of the Tang dynasty [i.e. Hongren and Huineng]. The chronicle traces the history of Buddhism in China from AD 60-774. Its contents and its significance as evidence for a Sichuan branch of Chan which honoured four patriarchs - Zhishen (609-701) to Wuzhu (714-74) - are examined inYanagida_1966: 278-349(summarized inDemieville_1970: 82). Yanagida 1983, an English translation of the introduction to his edition of P.2125, summarizes the history, literary features, and teachings of the chronicle and supplies a bibliography. Additional mss. segments include a nearly complete text in S.516/R.1, and partial texts on Or.8210/S.1611, Or.8210/S.5916, P.3717, and P.3727. Adamek is preparing a forthcoming English translation.
