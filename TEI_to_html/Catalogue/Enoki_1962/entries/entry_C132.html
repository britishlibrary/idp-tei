Section: R.1
Author: Chou Hsing-ssŭ
Ch'ien-tzŭ-wên
Ch. 86. II back, Scroll; 26 c. x 27 c.: 6 columns (4+2)+ll. 12 Tibetan.
In Chinese characters are inscribed the title, the name of the author with his official title, and the beginning of the Ch'ien-tzŭ-wên. For the Ch'ien-tzŭ-wên see Pelliot, Le Ts'ien tseu wen ou 'Livre du mille mots', T.P. XXIV, 1926, pp. 179-214, 293. Four MSS. of the work are available in the Stein collection at the British Museum (Giles, Descriptive Catalogue, nos. 2824, 7153, 7166, 7273).
The blank space before the Chinese title and between the title and beginning of the text has been filled by a Tibetan scribe, probably by way of writing practice, with brief notes on Buddhist topics (the eightfold way, etc.). The first two lines of Tibetan writing are, however, a transcription of the Chinese text:
tshyan dzi bun [...] chig [...] 'wen [...] 'gwa [...] san [...] gi [...] shi [...] lang [...] ce'u [...] hing [...] si [...] dzi [...] 'win [...] then [...] de [...] hwan [...] hwang [...] [note: The Chinese characters are given by the cataloguer.]
The Ch'ien-tzŭ-wên in Tibetan script in the Pelliot Collection at the Bibliothèque Nationale (P. 3419) is well known and has been published and studied by Haneda (Tôru), Bankan Taion Sen-jimon no dankan Tôyô Gakuhô, XIII, 1923, pp. 390-410 (with photographs of the text) and Tonkô Isho, 1926, Tokyo; by Lo Ch'ang-p'ei, T'ang Wu-tai Hsi-pei fang-yin (Academia Sinica. The National Research Institute of History and Philology Monographs, Ser. A, no. 12), Shanghai, 1933; and by Zaitsu (Aizô) Tonkôshutsudo kanzô taion no zairyô to Inkyô tono hikaku, Pt. 1, Tôŷôgaku Sôhen, vol. 1, 1934, Tokyo, pp. 105-53. Cf. also mentions made by Pelliot in J.A. XIX, 1912, pp. 584, 589-90, By Maspero in B.E.F.E.O. XX. ii, pp. 21, 32, 37, 41, 46-50, and by W. Simon in B.S.O.A.S. XXI. 2, 1958, p. 334.
