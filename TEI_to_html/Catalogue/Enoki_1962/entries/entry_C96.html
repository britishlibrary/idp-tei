Section: 2
Ch. 73. VII, frag. A. 7 (3); 13 columns (in three parts, 5+3+3).
May be a composition of the scribe himself written in vermillion by way of pastime. Consisits of three parts. Pt. 1: 'In the Garden of Ryagrodha (To-kên-shu-yûan) in the castle Kapila-vastu, Māṇava (Mo-na-p'o) gave birth to a daughter named Ming-yüeh. She will be able to make herself a queen to a great king even when she is of mean birth, if she has got on her palm such signs as, etc., etc.' Pt. 2: 'Prasenajit (Shêng-kuang), the Great, had two wives, Śrīmālā (Shêng-man) and Varśakara (Hsing-yü). Both of them were of such beauty as, etc., etc.' Pt. 3 explains the inevitability and permanence of relations between the cause and its effects.
