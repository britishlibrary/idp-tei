Section: R.1
Ch. 73. VIII. 13 (537): A. 7 columns Chinese interlinear transcription of the Tibetan; B. 1 column; C. 2 columns; D. (verso) 1 column.
(A) is a dhāraṇī concerning fifteen devas, including the Devas of the Four Directions (cāturmahā-rājikā-devāḥ). Sanskrit names of these devas are transcribed in Tibetan characters, to which Chinese characters of equivalent sound are given. As for the Devas of the Four Directions, the directions are written in Chinese, which do not appear in Tibetan script. Text reads as follows:
(1) OM ... [note: dhṛtarāṣṭrāya svāhā] tre ... su ...[?]... ra ... ya ... (for ...?) sba ... hA(?) .../OM ... [note: virūḍhakāya svāhā] b+hi ... ru ... dab ... ka ... ya ... sba ... hA(?) .../OM ... [note: virūpākṣāya svāhā] b+he ... da ...(crossed out) ru ... (2) bag ... k+sha ... ye ... sba ... hA(?) .../ba[?]/OM ... [note: vaiśravaṇāya svāhā] b+hi ... sha ...[?]... ra ... ma ... ne ... ye ... sba ... hA(?) .../ (3) OM ... [note: īśanāya svāhā] i ... sha ... na ...[?]... ye ... sba ... hA(?) .../OM ... [note: indrāya svāhā] yin ... dre ... ye ... sba ... hA(?) .../OM ... [note: agnaya svāhā] yag ... (4) ne ... [ye] ... sba ... hA(?) .../OM ... [note: nirṛitya svāhā] ni ... ri ... te ...[?]... ye ... sba ... hA(?) .../OM ... [note: varunāya svāhā] ba ... ru ... na ... ye ... sba ... hA(?) .../ (5) OM ... It is obvious that the transcription in Chinese characters is not always faithful to the Tibetan transcription.

Section: R.3
(C) A gāthā which is to be recited when one washes the images of Buddha. It resembles very much the gāthā in the Yü-hsiang kung-tê ching (Nanjio, no. 293: Taisho, XVI, no. 697, p. 799b(13-13) translated by Ratnacinta, which had been usually used in the medieval period in China (see Mochizuki, Bukkyô Daijiten, 1, p. 822(b-c)).
