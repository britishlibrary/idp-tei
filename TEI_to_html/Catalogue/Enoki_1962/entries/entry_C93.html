De-shing cung-tsong kyen-he
Ta-ch'êng chung-tsung chien-chieh
(Mahāyāna-mādhyamika-darśana)
Ch. 80. XI; 227 c. x 27 c.: 127 columns, with Tibetan transcriptions.
F. W. Thomas, S. Miyamoto, and G. L. M. Clauson, A Chinese Mahāyāna Catechism in Tibetan and Chinese Characters, J.R.A.S., 1929, pp. 37-76, where a photograph showing the initial 21 columns is published; S. Miyamoto, Daijô-chûsô-kenge oyobi sono kenkyû, Shûkyô kenkyû, New Series V. 4, 1929, pp. 103-28; Lo Ch'ang-p'ei, T'ang Wu-tai Hsi-pei fang-yin (Academia Sinica. The National Research Institute of History and Philology Monographs, Ser. A, no. 12), Shanghai 1933.
In this MS., Tibetan transcription is given to Chinese characters on the right side. A portion of this catechism appears in two other MSS, C94 and C131 which is written in Tibetan characters. Compare a facsimile reproduction and description of the MSS. in J.R.A.S., 1929, pp. 37 ff.
