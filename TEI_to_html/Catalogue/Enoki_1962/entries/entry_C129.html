Section: R.1
[Chin-kang pan-jo-po-lo-mi ching]
(Vajracchedikā-prajñāpāramitā-sūtra)
Vol. 72, fol. 72 and Fragment 32, two adjoining pieces of scroll; 62 c. x 31 c. and 16 c. x 18 c.: ll. 48 of Chinese in Tibetan script, dbu-med (a. ll. 1-38+parts of 39-40, 6 fragmentary and 24 more defective: b. parts of ll. 39-40+41-48, all defective at both ends).
F. W. Thomas and G. L. M. Clauson, A Chinese Buddhist Text in Tibetan Writing, J.R.A.S., 1926, pp. 508-26.
The text in Tibetan script is very close to the translation of Kumārajīva of the Taisho edition, but not exactly the same.
For the Chinese text's verso see C112 and 133.
