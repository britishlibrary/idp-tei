Liang-chou Yü-shan Kan-t'ung-ssŭ Shêng-yung t'ien-shang hsia-lai
(The portrait of the Emperor has come down from Heaven to the Yü-shan Kan-t'ung-ssŭ Temple in Liang-chou.)
Ch. 83. XI (754 part); 16 columns.
A statement in commemoration of the completion of the temple Kan-t'ung-ssŭ where the portrait of the first Emperor of Pei-chou (A.D. 556) was hung. The last column reads: 'Written by monk Tao-chao on the 22nd day of the 6th month of the 6th year of Ch'ien-tê (577)' ... In the statement it is said that they asked for the portrait in the 9th Enoki mentions that the portrait was requested in the 9th year of the Pao-ting, but the Pao-ting reign period (561-5) did not last more than five years.
For the other texts in this MS. see C1 and 98.
