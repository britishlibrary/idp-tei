Section: R.1
Hsiang-shu
'Book of physiognomy'
Ch. 87. XVI. 5 back; Scroll; 141 c. x 26 c.: 92 columns (last five incomplete): originally pasted on the back of Tibetan MS. 511.
Three MSS. of the same book are preserved in the Bibliothèque Nationale in Paris (P. 2527, 2797, 3589). Cf. Wang (Chung-min), Pa-li Tun-huang ts'an-chüan hsü-lu, Pt. 1, 1939, fasc. 3, pp. 6a-7b (= Tun-huang ku-chieh hsü-lu, Peking 1958, pp. 178-80). The present MS. of the Hsiang-shu, attributed to Hsü Fu and thirteen others of Han Dynasty, contains twenty-eight sections of the book, which properly consists of thirty-six sections. They are as follows:
1. Hsü (Preface)
2. Ch'ü-mao [note: Paris MS. ...] (Appearance)
3. Wu-kuan (Five organs)
4. Lu-fu (Six organs)
5. Mien (Face)
6. Fa (Hair)
7. Ê (Forehead)
8. Mei (Eyebrow)
9. Yen (Eyes)
10. Pi (Nose)
11. Êrh-lei (Ears)
12. Pi-jên-chung (Centre of upper lip)
13. Ch'un (Lips)
14. K'uo (Mouth)
15. Ch'ih (Teeth)
16. Shê (Tongue)
17. Shêng (Voice)
18. I-han (Jaw and chin)
19. Hsiang-yü-chên [note: P. ...] (Neck)
20. Pei [note: P. ...] (Back)
21. Hsin-hsieh [note: P. ...] (Breast)
22. Nai-ch'i [note: P. ...] (Female breast and navel)
23. Yü-ching ta-ch'i [note: P. ...] (Penis and testicles)
24. Pi-lai [note: P. ...] (Leg)
25. Huai-chiao (Foot)
26. Hsing-pu [note: P. ...] (Walk)
27. Pei-shou (Arm and hand)
28. [lacking] [?] ...
According to Wang Chung-min Paris MSS. have a character hsiang at the beginning of the name of each section, for instance .... The authors of the book, the present MS. says, are Hsü Fu, Li Ling, Tung Fang-shuo, Kuan Kung-ming, T'ao Yen, Kêng Kung, Chu Yün, [Ch'ien]-lou Hsien-shêng, Chang Liang, Lu Hsien-shêng, Shên-nung, Li Ku, and Chang Yü [P. ...], i.e. thirteen in all, while the Paris MSS. enumerate twelve, excluding Li Ku.
