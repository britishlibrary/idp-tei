Section: R.1
[Yu-p'o-sai-chieh ching, fasc. 5.]
(Upāsaka-śīla-sūtra)
Ch. 77. X. 13 (94); 88 columns. Written in a so-called pa-fên style and seems to be as early as Northern Wêi, i.e. very soon after the translation of the sūtra in A.D. 426 or 428 by Dharmarakṣa.
Contains the second half of the Tsa-p'in chih yü (chap. 19) and the first half of the Ching-san-kuei p'in (chap. 20). A fragment of the same sūtra (Fasc. 2, chaps. 10-11) dated A.D. 604 is in the British Museum (S. 4162). Cf. Giles, B.S.O.S. VIII, p. 4, and Descriptive Catalogue, no. 4088.
