C90
IOL Tib J 404

Section: R.1
Fragment 48 (404); 2 foll., 7+8 columns (lower halves missing).
Fol 1. Praise of the divya-cakṣus (...) of A-na-lü or Aniruddha. Somewhat resembles the Fo wu-po ti-tzŭ tzŭ-shuo pên-ch'i ching (A-na-lü p'in) (Nanjio, no. 729, Taisho, IV, p. 198).
Fol. 2. Praise of the dhūta or dhuta (...) of Ta-chia-yeh or Mahākāśyapa. Somewhat resembles the Fo wu-po ti-tzŭ tzŭ-shuo pên-ch'i ching (Ta-chi-yeh p'in) (Nanjio, no. 729, Taisho, IV, p. 190).
Fragment 48 apparently contained Chinese and Tibetan versions of the same text on alternate folios: the Chinese version of the third Tibetan text (Maudgalyāyana-stotra) is lacking.
