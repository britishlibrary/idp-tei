Lan-t'ing-chi
(Lan-t'ing hui-hs端)
(Lan-t'ing-chi-hs端)
Ch. 86. IV. 3, Scroll, 26 c. x 26 c.: 3+1 columns.
A part of the Lan-t'ing-chi (or Lan-t'ing hui-hs端 or Lan-t'ing-chi-hs端) of Wang Hsi-chih.
At the end Tibetan alphabets from ka to a are written three times probably for practice, and six Chinese characters (...) meaning room or house and corridor.
