Section: 1
To-len-to Pan. II. (E.) 7.8.07.
Prints 289-292 form four sections (first section on left, presumably print 288, is missing) of a five-part panorama, entitled 'Panoramic view taken from crest of watershed above Huo-ning-to Pass, showing portions of the Richtofen, To-lai-shan and Alexander III ranges of Nai-shan'. PUBLISHED: 'Ruins of Desert Cathay', vol. 2, p. 314. The explanatory text continues: 'The panorama was taken at an elevation of circ. 15,500 feet to the north-west of the pass, which itself is hidden by rocky foreground (D; see ii. pp. 314 sqq. and Map III). The view extends from the north-east (left) to the south-west (right); the entries above the panorama indicate only the approximate bearings. It shows portions of the Richtofen Range west (A) and east (B) of Hung-shui-pa Ho, rising to peaks of over 18,000 feet. The high peaks of the To-lai-shan Range, over which the pass leads, are masked by neighbouring ridges (C) [print 289]. Across the wide valley of the Pei-ta Ho are seen in the distance the Uge-shan group (E) [print 290], rising to close on 19,000 feet, and other snowy portions of the Alexander III Range (F to H) [prints 291-292]. The approximate position of the pass by which we crossed this range is indicated by (G) [extreme right of print 291]. For a view from the same point to the north-west see Fig. 237 [Photo 392/26 (388)].' Stein describes the taking of this view on p. 315: 'The atmosphere was delightfully clear, and with the pleasure of getting this chance for a photographic panorama I scarcely noticed the icy wind which played over our narrow arÃªte.
