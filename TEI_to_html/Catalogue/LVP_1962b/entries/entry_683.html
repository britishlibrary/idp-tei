Fragment of a treatise on the Tri-svabhāva or °lakṣaṇa (pariniṣpanna, paratantra, parikalpita). Compare Tri-svabhāva-nirdeśa, Bstan-ḥgyur, Mdo-ḥgrel, LVIII. 4, Cordier_1909_1915: p. 385.

'In what does the absence-of-own-nature (asvabhā-vatā) concerning the birth of things consist? In the fact that things depend upon others (paratantra-lakṣaṇa). How? Since things are caused by extraneous causes and do not exist through themselves....'
This passage seems to occur at the beginning of the Commentary on Triṃśaka-kārikā, Bstan-ḥgyur, Mdo-ḥgrel, LVIII. 1 and 10 (foll. 170-201b, Cordier_1909_1915: pp. 384 and 386.
