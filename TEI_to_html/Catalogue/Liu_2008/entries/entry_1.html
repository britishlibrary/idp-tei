Text
Calligraphy in clerical-script style, 118 columns. Fragment. Transcribed by Rao_1955

Provenance
Dunhuang caves. Removed in 1910 for Beijing. Ended up in the Fanjiang Ge 凡將閣 hall of Li Shengduo (Muzhai) 李盛鐸 (木齋 ) of Dehua 德化. After Li Shengduo died, the work was purchased in 1947 (dinghai) by Zhang Guchu 張谷雛 (1890-1968; i.e., Zhang Hong, a Cantonese painter working in an art publishing firm in the 1930s) [recorded in his "Jichuanan suo cang Dunhuang shishi tulu" 寄傳庵所藏敦煌石室圖案 manuscript copy]. Ye Gongchuo 葉恭綽 was one of the few people to see this work when it was in Li Shengduo's collection. After it went to Zhang Guchu,it was viewed by Huang Binhong黃賓虹 and Ye. When Rao Zongyi wrote about this piece, it was still in Zhang Guchu's collection. In 1960, it became part of the Peng family collection in Hong Kong, and it entered the John B. Elliott coll. in 1985.
