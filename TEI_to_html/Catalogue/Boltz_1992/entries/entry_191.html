Or.8210/S.516

Section: R.1
"This chronicle traces the history of Buddhism in China from 60-774. Its contents and its significance as evidence for a Sichuan branch of Chan which honoured four patriarchs-Zhishen (609-701) to Wuzhu (714-74)-are examined in Yanagida_1966: pp. 278-349 (summarized in Demieville_1970: p. 82). He dates the text to c. 774-81. Yanagida_1983, an English translation of the introduction to his edition of P.2125, summarizes the history, literary features, and teachings of the chronicle and supplies a bibliography. Additional mss. segments include Or.8210/S.1611, Or.8210/S.1776, Or.8210/S.5916, P.3717, and P.3727. It is collated with a transcription of its counterpart, P.2125, in T2075: T2075, vol.51.179a-196b. Adamek is preparing a forthcoming English translation."
