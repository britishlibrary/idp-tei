Section: R.1
"Archives of Hare Garden, part one with preface. Early bibiographies ascribe this work to Du Sixian (). The title recorded at the close of this fragment is followed by a colophon in a different - and poor - hand. This segment of the text encompasses five headings: 1) Differentiating the Celestial and Terrestial Realms (bian tian di); 2) Rectifying Calendrical Tabulations (zheng li shu); 3) deliberating about "fengshan" (yi feng shen); 4) quelling eastern tribes (zheng dong yi); 5) equalizing prefectural lands (jun zhou rang). The popularity of this children's manual of instruction is attested in an episode of "Bei meng suo yan" 19. GuoChangcheng_1984 concludes it was compiled no later that the reign of the Tang emperor Zhaozong (r. 888-904) and provides a recension of the text, based on a collation of this ms., Or.8210/S.1086, Or.8210/S.1722, and P.2573."
