Or.8210/S.705

Section: R.1
"Translated by Giles as 'Important elementary teaching for the young', this is the earliest dated copy (851, see colophon) of the popular children's textbook in the Stein collection. None of the Dunhuang mss. in other collections are known to be earlier. Additional segments of this quadrisyllabic aide-memoire include Or.8210/S.1308, Or.8210/S.5431, Or.8210/S.5449, Or.8210/S.5463, Or.8210/S.5464, Or.8210/S.5513, Or.8210/S.5584, Or.8210/S.6128, Or.8210/S.6131, and Or.8210/S.6224. The complete text, found in P.2487, P.2578, P.3054, and P.3610, has 348 lines and 1,392 characters. The title is not found in the bibliographies of the Sui and Tang histories. The entry for P.2487 ascribes the text to Ma Renshou, according to a citation in P.2721."
