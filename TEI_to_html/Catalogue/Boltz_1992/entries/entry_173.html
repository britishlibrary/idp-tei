Or.8210/S.381

Section: R.4
"Transcribed in LMS: LMS. Two quatrains are recorded. The initial heptasyllabic quatrain corresponds to the 'Ming zhong ji' ("Gaatha" on the Ringing Bell) inscribed at the close of the story of monk Zhixing in Or.8210/S.1625 (see also Or.8210/S.381). The second pentasyllabic quatrain opens with the line, 'If you do not arise from slumber when you hear the bell'. Following it is an anonymous copyist's date, 26th of fourth month of Xiantong 14 (873)."
