Or.8210/S.329

Section: R.1
"This fragment and Or.8210/S.361 originally formed one unit which, as ZhaoHeping_1990 observes in his transcription and study of the text, may be considered a lost work compiled by Du Youjin no later than 747. Recorded here are models for wedding congratulations, requesting a favour, presenting draft horses and bond servants, inquiring after ailments, expressing gratitude for inquiries after ailments, New Year and winter solstice greetings, acknowledging the death of parents, aunt and wife, and for noting injury to a dog or horse. The closing selection in a lengthy congratulation in celebration of overcoming tribes in the nw. Ebrey_1985: 597-98 outlines the contents of fragments of this text in P.3637 and P.3849. See BST: 429 for further studies."
