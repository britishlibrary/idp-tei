Section: R
[Samādhirājasūtra]
Written in the southern Turkestan Brāhmī (main type). Three times the Central Asian vowel -e is written with a long stroke and a flag-shaped hook to the right, but only once apparently because the Indian -e otherwise would have touched the preceding anusvāra. Five times the looped diacritic sign for -i is written. Cf. the description in AKh I, p. 297: “D. VI.1. MS. on paper; Brāhmī; No. 28. Note xv. Lefthand fragment, 4 1/2" 3", with string-hole, of a leaf numbered 70. Full size, probably 18" 3". Language: Sanskrit. Characters: upright Gupta of seventh or eighth century. Contents: apparently some Buddhist canonical work; on obverse, end of section 43, the beginning of the 44th section being Smarāmy-ahaṃ kalpāṅs-atīta ..., on reverse, end of section 51. [Dr. Hoernle.].” To the same folio belongs Hoernle Or.6403B2(2) [M 3] which contains two fragments which are fitted in a sheet of paper on which a first transliteration (by A.F. Rudolf Hoernle) is written. The right fragment is given here in bold, the left fragment in italics. The text differs considerably from the Sanskrit text known so far [cf. SRS(D) II, pp. 256-259: chapter 17, verses 142c-157c] and also from the Tibetan translation. For the Chinese translation cf. T 639, 15.572a28-b29.
