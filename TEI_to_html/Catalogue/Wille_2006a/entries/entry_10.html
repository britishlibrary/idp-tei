Kha.i.189

Section: R
[Śūraṃgamasamādhisūtra]
There are three fragments, the script is the early southern Turkestan Brāhmī. Cf. Ser III 1437: “Kha. i.189. [...] (1) three frs. of ś ūraṅgamasamādhi (Dṛḍhamati).” 170.1 In the Tibetan translation the text of the fragment corresponds to Peking Kanjur, section Mdo, vol. thu (70), fol. 286a1-b6 = TT 800, vol. 32, p. 75.1.1-2.6; Derge[Taipei] Kanjur, section Mdo sde, vol. da (11), foll. 263a2-b5 = No. 132, vol. 11, p. 526.523.2-524.5. In the Chinese translation of Kumārajīva the corresponding passage is found in T 642, 15.631c (translation Śgs § 21, Nos. 83-100). In verso 5f. the remains of some ślokas are preserved. Cf. Śgs, p. 119, note 43: “In the Tibetan translation, § 21 is followed by a partly versified passage going from folios 286b5-289a3. These stanzas probably did not appear in the original Sanskrit used by Kumārajīva as he certainly would not have omitted to translate them.”
