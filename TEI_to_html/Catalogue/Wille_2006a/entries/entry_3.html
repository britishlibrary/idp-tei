[Saddharmapuṇḍarīkasūtra]
Written in the southern Turkestan Brāhmī (main type). Six times the Central Asian vowel -e is written, but only once apparently because the Indian -e otherwise would have touched the preceding superscribed vowel. There are two separated fragments of Kha. i.60. Fragment Or.8212/25(3) is given here in italics and Kha. i.209 in bold. BB 33, pp. 129f., No. 46]; KN 87.8(?)- 90.2; Wa 38.6(ḥ)-39.33, 214.25(?)-216.20.
