Buddhist religious book of 63 ff., 5 1/4 in. wide and 5 3/4 in. high.

Colophon (fol. 46b): On the 4th day of the 6th month of the 10th year of Cu Cing, the year of the tiger, Xulutmak, who is living in the city of Uc-luk-cug (?), copied (this text) by order of Asudai Orul, who has newly learned (Buddhism?) and is the governor of Salir (-uirur?). Dadhu! adgu!
The 10th year of Cu Cing, 'tiger year', corresponds to A.D. 1350. Cu Cing is probably a corrupt form of zhi zheng of the Yuan Dynasty.)
