Section: 1
'phags pa don yod pa'i zhags pa'i snying po'i gzungs
[Ārya-amoghapāśahṛdaya-dhāraṇī]
Incipit ( 1r.1 ): @//_!_//'phags pa don yod pa'i zhags pa'I snyIng po'i gzungs log shig tu phyungs te/
A complete copy of the Amoghapāśahṛdaya-dhāraṇī --not the sutra, but the dhāraṇī spell itself, extracted from the wider dhāraṇī sutra, as made explicit in the opening line which says the spell has been "extracted for recitation" ( 1r.1 log shig tu phyungs). The spell is translated where possible into Tibetan. In this way the present item resembles ITJ_312/4 and ITJ_372/2 , which are also copies of the same Amoghapāśahṛdaya-dhāraṇī spell with as much translated as possible. In this regard, see also ITJ_322 and ITJ_348 , which are translations of the Uṣṇīṣavijaya-dhāraṇī spell. Note that the Dunhuang version of the Amoghapāśahṛdaya-dhāraṇī sutra also translates parts of the spell, but with several more passages left in transliteration. The Dunhuang version of the sutra (for a complete copy, see PT_49/4 ) is clearly a different translation than the canonical edition (compare P_366 ), though the two translations do seem to be based on similar Sanskrit originals. The principal differences between the two versions concern the spell proper; compare P_366: 258b.1- 260a.2 with either the present item or PT_49/4: 86-117 . Finally, note that the colophon to the Dunhuang version of the text seen in PT_49/4 specifies that the work includes both the dhāraṇī text and the ritual manual ( cho ga ) associated with that dhāraṇī . Though not distinguished as such, the same ritual manual is also found in the canonical version ( P_366: 260a.3- ). It may be that the ritual manual initially travelled alongside the dhāraṇī , and then was subsumed into the dhāraṇī itself.

Explicit ( 5r.4 ): Om dza ya hung phad swA hA//_!_//_//rdzogs s+ho//_!_//_!_//

Section: 4
'du shes bcu bstan pa'i mdo
[Daśa-saṃjñā-nirdeśa-sūtra]
Incipit ( 20.1 ): @//_!_//rgya gar skad du /da sha sang ngId nyA nI ra de sha su tra//
On the occasion of his parinirvāṇa , the Buddha gives a brief teaching on the ten consciousnesses that manifest during the dying process. The colophon may be in two hands. The first part explains that the present collection of texts is the daily practice ( yi dam ) of one Kwag dar ma . The second part says it is the pledge of 'ang btsun shes rab dpal .
