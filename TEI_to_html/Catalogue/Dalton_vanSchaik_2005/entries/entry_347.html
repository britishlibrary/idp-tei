Incipit ( V1.1 ): @/de ltar de bzhin du/
A somewhat garbled account of an empowerment ritual. The hand is the same as ITJ_384 , which is a series of rituals related to the Sarvadurgatipariśodana-tantra . The two manuscripts may be closely related, as the present text also seems to be related to the Sarvadurgatipariśodana . In both manuscripts, the empowerment ritual is performed for the benefit of a patron ( yon bdag ). The ritual in the present text is refered to as the subsequent empowerment ( rjes dbang ), and may be intended as a continuation of the rituals of ITJ_384 . The ritual involves making offerings to a number of non-Buddhist Indic deities, including Brahmā and Indra . Other groups of deities known from the maṇḍala of the Sarvadurgatipariśodana appear here as the objects of the offerings, including ten protectors of the directions, eight great nāga s, eight great planets, and twenty-eight lunar mansions.
