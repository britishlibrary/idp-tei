Section: 1
Incipit ( Rf.1r.1 ): par rdzogs pa'i sangs rgyas dang /
This concertina manuscript is incomplete, so it is not possible to tell whether the recto and verso contain separate texts or parts of a continuous text. Here they are dealt with separately. The text on the recto is a confession prayer addressed to the Buddhas and bodhisattvas, without any tantric terminology.

Section: 2
Incipit ( Vf.1r.1 ): 'o/_/de bzhin gshegs pa thms cad kyi thugs kyi phyag rgya yongs
This text describes the effects of a series of mūdra s. The first four are the mūdra s of the body, speech, mind and vajra of the Bhagavat s. This is followed by the vajra fist, the vajra of the great samaya ( Vf.2v.2 rdo rje khu tshur dam tshig chen po 'i rdo rje), the smile ( Vf.2v.3 rab tu 'dzum) and the solid vajra ( Vf2v.4 rdo rje sra ba).

Explicit ( Vf.2v.4 ): /de bzhin rab tu 'dzum zhing rdo rje sra ba 'I phyag rgya dngos grub tham+s
