Section: 1
'phags pa spyan ras gzigs gyi dbang phyug gi mtshan brgya rtsa brgyad
a rgya pA lo kyI te shwa ra sya na ma a sha sha na ka na
[Ārya-avalokiteśvarasya-nāma-aṣṭaśaka]
rgyal ba rin po che za ma tog
Incipit ( Rf.1r.1 ): @/_/rgya gar skad du//_//a rgya pA lo kyI te shwa ra sya na ma a sha sha na ka na//_/
This concertina is written in two different hands on the recto and verso respectively and is missing several folios. Folios Rf.1r , Rf.1v and Rf.2r are each separated by a lacunae of several folios. Two further lacunae are found between Rf.3v and Rf.4r , and between Rf.6r and Rf.6v . The manuscript is also incomplete at the end.
The Ārya-avalokiteśvara-nāma-aṣṭaśaka is one of the most popular tantric texts in the Tibetan Dunhuang collections. For a discussion of the text see ITJ_351/3 . The text in this manuscript is very similar to the canonical edition. While the beginning and end are present, most of the text is missing, there being significant lacunae between the first, second and third folio sides.

Explicit ( Rf.2v.5 ): /rgyal ba thams shad [sic] gyi rin po che za ma tog

(Rf3r.1)

cis [sic] bya ba rdzogs so//

Section: 3
ltung bshags
Incipit ( Vf.1r.1 ): sangs rgyas thams cad gsol ba 'debs so/
This is the last lines of a Mahāyāna prayer of confession. The same text is found in many other Dunhuang manuscripts, usually with the full title Byang chub ltung bzhags .

Explicit ( Vf.1r.4 ): //ltung bshags rdzogs so//
