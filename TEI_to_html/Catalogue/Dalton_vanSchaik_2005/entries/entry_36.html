Section: 1
[lha klu chen po rnams spyan drang ba]
[rgyud gsum pa]
Incipit ( Vf.1r.1 ): ...rgyal chen lha dang klu/
The LVP catalogue applied two shelfmarks to this manuscript--316 and 525. LVP: 525 applies to most, but not all, of the recto ( Rf.4r - Rf.29v ), while LVP: 316 applies to the rest of the manuscript. Today these two parts are found together under the single British Library shelfmark IOL Tib J 316.
This prayer (incomplete at the beginning) invites the mundane protectors of the teachings, beginning with Brahma and continuing with many other non-Buddhist deities. The prayer often appears at the beginning of a collection of prayers, as is the case here. An additional four line verse of exhortation to listen to the recitations that follow is appended in this version. A detailed commentary on the prayer is found at ITJ_711 .

Explicit ( Vf.2v.1 ): bar bzhed pa 'dir gshegs shig//

Section: 5
Incipit ( Rf.4r.1 ): @/_/phyogs bcu'I sangs rgyas thams cad la phyag 'tshal lo/
This text begins as a series of homages to the buddhas of the ten directions, similar to ITJ_320 . There follows ( 18r.3 ) an exhortation to perform various kinds of worship and offering to these buddhas, and to make paintings ( ri mo ) of them. Then immediately after this is a prayer in which the supplicant is to insert his or her own name ( Rf.18v.1 bdag myIng 'dI zhes). The prayer is directed against the three bad rebirths (as animal, hell-being or hungry ghost). The end of the prayer is missing due to a lacuna between Rf.21v and Rf.22r .

Explicit ( Rf21v.3 ): //byang chub mchog gI sems de gzhar
