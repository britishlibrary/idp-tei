Incipit ( 1v.1 ): @//sI na hri hō kyam ko chod hyan 'hwad myan 'dze 'dzu ngu shIg ko hyan//
A long series of mantra s, most not specific to a deity, though the names of deities occasionally appear, for example, Jambhla on 4r.1 . The mantra s on 7v-8r mention the five buddha families in turn. An image on 5v-6r contains, among other elements, a wheel of weapons under a trident, surrounded by flames. Around the wheel of weapons, the four cardinal points are marked with Om , while the four ordinal points are marked with Ka , Wa , Ya and Ri .
The hand in which the text is written is highly ornamental, and there is are a number of ornamental designs such as conch shells ( 4r.3 ) and ornamented syllables ( 4v.1 ). The same hand is found in ITJ_530 , PT_70/1 , and PT_311 , all of which are collections of mantra s or dhāraṅī s
