Incomplete at end, with one folio missing in middle. Complete at beginning, as is clear when the present item is compared to ITJ_420/2 , which is another version of the same work. Three sections may be distinguished, with the first ending at 2r.1 , and the second at 4v.5 . The first section consists of a series exhortations and invitations for the gods, nƒÅgas , demons and spirits. The second section contains a series of prayers to accompany the general offering of bali ( gtor ma ) cakes. After the initial prayers, the practitioner is instructed to recite a mantra seven times, after which come the dedicatory prayers. Repeated references are made to the sponsor of the rite (yon bdag), and an unnamed scripture ( gzhung ) is cited on 6v.2-3 . The third section, which is incomplete, is the ransom offering ( glud gtor ) for karmic creditors ( lan chags ). In this way, the offering rite described here is another example of the standard three-part bali offering seen in a number of Dunhuang manuscripts (see for example ITJ_573 and PT_36 ).
