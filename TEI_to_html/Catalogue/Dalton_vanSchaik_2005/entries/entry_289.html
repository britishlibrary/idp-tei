Incipit ( Rf.1r.1 ): nI don myI che'i//mtshan ma myed par chud pa nyId byang cub du bsngos pa yin ni/_/yang kha cig na re/dang po sems
Compare ITJ_707/1 and ITJ_1372 . This is a page from a version of the much-studied Tibetan Chan work, PT_116/5: 126.3-129.3 , containing a number of quotations from a variety of sutras. The fragment expresses a strong preference for a non-conceptual and immediate ( cig car ) approach to contemplation over a gradual ( rim gyis ) one, even for beginners on the path. The passages cited from the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra and the Lankāvatāra Sūtra ( P_775 ) are also found in STMG . The other work cited--the Yang dag pa'i ye shes kyi yon tan bsam gyis mi khyab pa'i mdo ( Tathāgataguṇajñānācintyaviṣayāvatāranirdeśa , P_852 --passage not found) are not cited in STMG . An English translation of this fragment may be found in Faber 1985: 55-58 . Further readings include: Tanaka 1992 , Ueyama 1983 .
