Section: 1
Ngan btsong rnam par sbyang ba'i dkyil 'khor bsgo bzhi bstan pa
Incipit ( R1.1 ): @//___//yon gyi bdag po mying 'di bzhis bgyi ba'i/
This text is a ritual for a deceased person ( tshe 'das pa ): at various points in the text the name of the dead person is to be inserted into a prayer. The ritual describes a four-gated maṇḍala . For each gate the Sanskrit name of the direction is given in a rough Tibetan phonetic transcription. Then, for each gate, a prayer ( smon lam ) is given to be recited on behalf of the deceased. Each of the prayers addresses Śakyamūni , the eight great Uṣṇīṣa ,"and so on," suggesting the deities of the Śakyamūni maṇḍala of the Sarvadurgatipariśodhana-tantra . At the end of each prayer the buddha specific to that direction is invoked, along with one of the five wisdoms.
This manuscript is incomplete at the end. A more complete version of the same text is found in PT_37/2 . The present manuscript, which is well-written, may be a fair copy taken from PT_37/2 , which is very roughly written. The present manuscript reproduces several errors from that version, and adds some more errors which seem to be due to misreadings of PT_37/2 .
