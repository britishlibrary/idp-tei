Treatise on the samaya vows. Some lines are written in red ink, which may indicate that this is a commentary on a treatment of samaya in another source, perhaps a tantra. The three vows are: [i] not ceasing the practice of yi dam , mantra and mūdra , [ii] not disseminating the secret instructions to others and [iii] not developing bad thoughts toward the vajra master or the vajra brothers and sisters. In an interesting geographical reference, the highest form of unceasing continuity is compared to the flow of the Gtsang po river. Another aspect of the three roots is the injunction not to develop ill-will toward the master or the vajra brothers and sisters. Secrecy is also stressed. These vows seem to be closest to the Mahāyoga version of the samaya . An awareness of other samaya systems is shown in the closing line, which states that if these three roots of samaya are kept, there is no need to add any other samaya vows.

A description of the deities of a maṇḍala . The deities are the group of eight animal-headed goddesses which can also be found in ITJ_716/V1 and ITJ_332/1 . Their names are given in corrupted Sanskrit transliteration. The maṇḍala realm is repeatedly referred to as a "vast field" (zhing chen).
