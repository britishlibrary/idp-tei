Section: 1
Incipit ( R1.1 ): rin po che pad ma'i
This manuscript is a sheet (probably originally a scroll) which has been folded down its length. The original recto of the scroll, containing Chinese text, has been folded inside, and the outer part has been used to write the Tibetan document. The other part of this manuscript is in the Pelliot collection: PT_320A: recto should be placed before this manuscript, and PT_320B: verso should be placed after it. The text on PT_320B: recto is not directly related to this one. There is probably a lacuna between the end of PT_320A: recto this manuscript.
This is a sādhana of Avalokiteśvara which would probably fall into the category of the Yoga tantra s. It seems to belong to the same group as the sādhana found in PT_331 and the sādhana found in a manuscript split between the Stein and Pelliot collections: ITJ_583/2 and PT_288: recto . This version of the sādhana is lacking the description of the maṇḍala and consort.
The extant text starts on PT_320A: recto , although the original beginning of the manuscript seems to be lost. By comparison with the other sādhana s mentioned above, we can see that this manuscript comes in at the point where the three syllables are visualized: an Ōṃ at the crown of the head, an Aṃ at the throat and a Hūng at the heart. This visualization establishes the emptiness of self and phenomena. Then the celestial palace is established on a sun disc from the syllable phrung. Then on a moon disc from the syllable Hri the meditator appears as Avalokiteśvara , with a white body. The deity's accoutrements are described here in more detail than in the other versions of the sādhana : he wears a "lion robe" (seng gos) with gold tracery, and a ba hi skirt. He wears a crown (dbu rgyan), armlets (dpung rgyan), a necklace (se mo do) and earrings (snyan ca). This is the end of PT_320A: recto.
The following section of the text appears in this manuscript. Avalokiteśvara is sitting cross-legged on the lotus, with the three syllables mentioned earlier at his three places. Binding the samayamūdra , one recites the root mantra (not given here, but see ITJ_583/2 ), and the jñānasattva appears, making the meditator one with space (dbyings) and identical with Avalokiteśvara 's vajra body, speech and mind. At this point, this manuscript comes to an end, and the rest of the text appears on PT_320B: recto .
The meditator develops great pride in the identity with the deity. At his heart is a moon disc upon which is the mantra garland. The is to be recited 1008 times or more, along with "the three suchnesses" (de kho na nyid gsum). The final part of the practice is introduced with the phrase "If you wish to dismiss..." (gtong bar 'dod na). The same phrase appears in ITJ_583: Rf.5v.3-4 . The following practice is also described in general terms similar to the other text, although here there is a further conclusion to the meditation. The meditator is very briefly instructed to invite the deities, do the mūdra s, venerate them with the songs and dances, and do the confession and aspirational prayer. Then the deity and celestial palace dissolve into light and into the meditator's body, and he meditates without conceptualization. Finally, the meditator is enjoined to develop the deity by remembering the syllable, to protect the body with the mantra and mūdra s. The text ends with a mantra: Oṃ ba dzra yag sha kro dha khā dha khā dha hūng phat.
