Incipit ( 1r.1 ): @//sa bdag ched po tsang kun zhes bya ba nI
Fragment from a manual for preparing a ritual site. The manual discusses various local spirits ( sa bdag ) of the Tibetan landscape, and where they can be expected to dwell according to the year or the season. This information is used in order to determine where to dig into the earth. Thus it is an early version of the rite described in later manuals; compare for example the Śrīśambaramaṇḍalavidhi by the twelfth century master Vibhūticandra ( P_2226: 334a.5- ). The present item is of particular interest because, unlike later presentations of the rite, it names specific Tibetan spirits and makes use of the Sino-Tibetan animal calendar.
