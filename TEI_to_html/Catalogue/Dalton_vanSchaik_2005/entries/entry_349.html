Section: 1
Incipit ( V1.1 ): @/:/na mo rad na tra ya ya/na ma sh+can d+ha badzra pa na ye ma ha yag k+sha se na pa ta ye/
This dhāraṇī spell is written on the verso of a Chinese scroll. The dhāraṇī has not been identified, although it shares its opening lines with the spell in the bdud rtsi thabs sbyor gyi snying po bzhi pa zhes bya ba'i gzungs ( P_419 ). The deity addressed in the opening syllables appears to be Caṇḍavajrapāṇi . This name appears in one sādhana in the bstan 'gyur ( P_5162 ).

Explicit ( V1.17 ): tI sh+Ta ti sh+Ta/b+han d+ha b+han d+ha/ha na ha na/a mrI te hum phat/

Incipit ( V2.1 ): @//Id ti mid ti ti li mid ti ti li mi li mid ti li mi li la ma li ti la mi/
This dhāraṇī spell is written further along the verso of the Chinese scroll. It bears close, though not exact, resemblance to a spell from the Mahāmayūrīvidyārājñī ( P_178: 92a.7-92b.3 ).
