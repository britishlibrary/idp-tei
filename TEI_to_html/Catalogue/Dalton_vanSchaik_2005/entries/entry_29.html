Incipit ( Rf.1r.1 ): pa'i gdos pa' dang mtshan ma cis kyang myi gos pa' lags so//
A general discussion of the tantric system associated with the wrathful deity Dpal chen po . The deity has three heads and six arms, his consort is named Krodhīśvarī , and he is depicted trampling on the Hindu gods, Mahādeva and Umadeva . His maṇḍala is generated from the seed syllable, hūṃ, and seems to belong to the buddha family of Amitābha ( snang ba mtha' yas , see Rf.6v.1). An unfortunately unidentified root text (lung) is cited on how the deity only seems to be wrathful on the outside. In addition, a text called Mye'i lung is quoted in the context of describing the fires surrounding the deity.
