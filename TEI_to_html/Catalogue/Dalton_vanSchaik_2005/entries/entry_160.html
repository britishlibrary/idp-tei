gser 'od dam pa'i skrags pa'i yi ge
[gser 'od dam pa'i rnga sgra bsgrags pa'i yi ge]
su ba rna pra myi ta 'ka'
[Suvarṇaprabhā-mṛdaṅga]
Incipit ( Rf.1r.1 ): rgya gar skad du/ su ba rna pra myi ta 'ka'
This prayer does not appear in the bka' 'gyur or bstan 'gyur , but is well-represented in the Dunhuang manuscripts. The other manuscript copies (all in the Pelliot collection) sometimes have truncated titles, but all appear to be the same text.

Explicit ( Rf.6v.5 ): /gser 'od mchog la phyag tshal stod/_/ rtsogs [sic]+ so

Section: 2
dgyod sangs gyi smon lam
Incipit ( Rf.7r.1 ): @/_/ dgon chog sum [sic] la phyag tshal lo/_/ gyod sangs [sic] gyi smon lam du gsol ba/
This prayer of purification through reprentance is the same as the one found in ITJ_208 . It is not the same as the gser 'od dam pa'i gyod tshangs ( ITJ_450 ), despite the similarities in the name and idiosyncratic orthography. As well as confession, the prayer is concerned with making offerings, and instructions on making the offerings are interspersed with the prayers.

Explicit ( Vf.3r.6 ): / dgyod sangs [sic] gyi smon lam rdzogs so!!!!

Section: 3
snang ba mtha yas gyI yon tan 'byor pa stan pa'
sung snang ba tha yas
Incipit ( Vf.3v.1 ): @//snang ba mtha [sic] yas gyI yon tan 'byor pa stan pa'//
Prayer to Amitābha and the aspiration to be reborn in his pure land ( zhing mchog ). Contains a reference to "the maṇḍala of sameness" ( Vf5r.v mnyam pa nyId gyis dkyil 'khor) and to "the maṇḍala of dharma" ( Vf6v.1 chos gyI dkyil 'khor). The prayer states ( Vf8r.3-4 ) that rebirth in the pure land will be attained by renouncing the four wrong views (phyin ci log bzhi) and meditating on the path of non-duality (gnyis su myed pa'i lam). The theme of non-duality permeates the prayer, which in this respect is similar to the Dunhuang Amitābha prayers transcribed in Silk_1993 . Apart from this, the prayers are different. They are also not related to the Amitābha mindfulness prayer found in the bka' 'gyur ( P_154a ).
