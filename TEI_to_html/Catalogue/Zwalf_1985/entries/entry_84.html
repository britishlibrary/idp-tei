IOL Khot S 46

Section: 1
Incantations and other esoteric sutras
Scroll of dharani and sutras in Sanskrit and Khotanese. Upright Gupta script. Ink on paper, headpiece of gouache on cloth. The six texts in this scroll form a ritual collection of esoteric sutras. At the beginning is a rectangle of cloth painted with two geese standing on lotuses holding stems of lotus buds in their mouths. The effect resembles the later Islamic 'unvan, or headpiece opening a text, and like the latter doubteless derives from later classical andByxantine headpieces. There are four colophons, giving a date equivalent to AD 943.
