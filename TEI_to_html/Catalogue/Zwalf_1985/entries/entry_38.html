Section: R.1
Rules for Monks
Scroll of Pratimoksasutra in Chinese. Ink on paper. AD 406. This scroll, the earliest dated manuscript found at Dunhuang, was written only fifty years after the first cave temple was excavated, and the copier, a monk, apologies for his handwriting hoping that no-one will laugh at it. The text sets out the Pratimoska of the Sarvastivadin School which flourished in Gandhara and north-west Inida. Kashmiri monks, including Punyatrata who translated this particular work, translated Sarvastivadin literature into Chinese in the late 4th century.
