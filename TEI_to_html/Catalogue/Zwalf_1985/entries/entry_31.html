Section: R.1
The Book of Middle-length Sayings
Scroll of Zhong a han jing ( Madhyamagamasutra ) in Chinese. Ink on paper. This title corresponds to the Pali Majjhimanikeya , or collection of middle-length sutras, and treats of the Buddha's early life and the Four Noble Truths. It was translated by a monk from Kashmir, Samghadeva , in the late 4th century.
Paper, invented in China, was common use by the 5th century AD. The paper scroll follows an earlier form of document where inscribed bamboo slips were tied together and rolled up for storage. Sheets of paper were pasted together and backed to form a scroll.
