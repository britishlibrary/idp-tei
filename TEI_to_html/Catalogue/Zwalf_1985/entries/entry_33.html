The Book of Kindred Sayings
From a stupa at Khadalik , Xinjiang, China. Pothi manuscript of Samyuktagama in Sanskrit. Fragments of 15 folios. Gupta script. Ink on birch bark. The Samyuktagama is the Sanskrit version of the Samyuttanikaya , the third of the four Pali books of the Buddha's sermons. It owes its name to the grouping of its sutras. The Pali version which, unlike the Sanskrit, is complete, comprises 2,889 sutras. This Sarvastivada version was known only in Chinese and Tibetan until parts were discovered in Central Asia . This now fragmentary manuscript was written in the Himalayan region on birch bark, the usual writing material for that area, before being taken to Central Asia.
