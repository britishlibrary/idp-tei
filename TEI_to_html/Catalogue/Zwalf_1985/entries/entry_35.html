Section: R
The Book of Exalted Utterances
Pothi manuscript of Udanavarga in Sanskrit. Slanting Gupta script. Ink on paper. The Udanavarga is a Sarvastivadin compilation, attributed to Dharmatrata (1st century AD?), of popular ethical verse maxims. It significantly overlaps with an old north-west Indian (Gandhari) Dharmapada and Pali canonical Dharmmapada which belongs to the Khuddakanikaya or Minor Tradition, the collection of mainly verse texts not considered as spoken by the Buddha but sufficiently ancient to have entered the Canon before the first schisms. The script suggests a scribe from Kucha
