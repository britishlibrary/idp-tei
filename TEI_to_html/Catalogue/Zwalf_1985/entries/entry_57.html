Section: 1
Incantation of the Sutra of Eternal Life
Pothi manuscript of Aparaimutayuhsutrasharani in Khotanese. Upright Gupta script. Ink on paper, with 1 miniature in gouache. 20 folios. This brief text is a dharani, or incantation, explaining the rewards gained by reciting the sutra of its title. Like all works it repeats endlessly the same basic formulae, varying only the number of persons, times, or years involved. Whoever recites the sutra in praise of Aparmimutayuh, or has it written, will repeatedly have his life extended by 100 years, until he finally dies he will obtain rebirth in the paradise (buddhaksetra) of Aparimitayuh. Although the basic texts of the Pure land School are Indian in origin, its ideas attracted little interest in India itself, no doubt because fo the discrepancies between its sensual paradise and the orthodox Middle Way.
At the beginning of this manuscript is a small roundel of two concentric ciircles with a coloured seated Buddha (apparently Amitabha) superimposed against the inner circle. Amitayuh and Aparimitayuh mean the same thing - Endless Life - and are the alternative names fo the Buddha Amitabha (Endless Radiance). Khotanese was the anicent language of western Xinjiang and has now been identified as one of the Saka or Scythian languages of the Iranian group.
