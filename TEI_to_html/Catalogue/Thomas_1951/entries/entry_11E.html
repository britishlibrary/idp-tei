"[ll. 1-3]...having on the last occasion become one srang of copper, it was arranged that not later than...of the Bird-year one srang of copper should be delivered. [ll. 3-5] If at that time payment should not have been made, from one srang it should become two; and even if it should be seized from the door of Li Rme'u Bying la gsas, it was agreed that there should be no word [of protest]. On Phyug mtshams as a Thousand-district of Mdo gams see p. 106 (Ch.9.i.37, IOL Tib J 751), vol, 1 Thomas_1935: p. 279. The surnames Li and Dar are instanced in the consolidated list infra (p. 113 sqq.); Rma has occured supra (p. 20: Fragment.67, IOL Tib J 1126); Rhul po occurs p. 215 (M.Tagh.0050); Tsi bra (for Tsib ra?) is not known, but is, no doubt, name of some district.
