JOHN BURKE, Gap between Bala Hissar and Bala Burj Kabul, 1880.
The strategic significance of the saddle between the upper Bala Hissar and the ridge of the Sher Darwaza mountain (on the right of this photograph), was noted in Mughal accounts of sieges of the citadel. In the centre of this photograph can be seen the water channel that followed the contour above the city from the west.
