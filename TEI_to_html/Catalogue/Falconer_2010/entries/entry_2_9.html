JAMES RATTRAY, Afghaun foot soldiers in their winter dress, with entrance to the Valley of Urgundeh, 1842.
Rattray describes how in 1842 the British met with considerable resistance from fighters at this rocky watchtower between the valleys of Maidan and Argandeh. A force of these ‘wild, unruly, merry fellows,’ was however raised for service with the British.
Lithograph from James Rattray, Scenery, Inhabitants and Costumes, Afghaunistan (London, 1848), British Library.
