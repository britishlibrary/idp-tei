JAMES RATTRAY, Chief Executioner and assistant of His Majesty the late Shah, c. 1841–2.
These two figures were part of a large number of guards and executioners in Shah Shuja’s retinue. The chief executioner, Muhammad Shah, is seated, and Rattray describes him as ‘a gentleman by birth, who rode before the sovereign. In his hand is the painted wand, surmounted by a bird, indicative of his being the head of a family.’
Lithograph from James Rattray, Scenery, Inhabitants and Costumes, Afghaunistan (London, 1848), British Library
