JAMES RATTRAY, Temple of Ahmed Shah, King of Afghaunistan, Kandahar, c. 1841-2.
The mausoleum of Ahmad Shah Durrani, founder of the modern Afghan state, is shown surrounded by visitors, with a line stretching back towards a fort in the background. Under striped awnings, vendors sell the melons for which Qandahar is famous to this day.
Lithograph from James Rattray, Scenery, Inhabitants and Costumes, Afghaunistan (London, 1848), British Library.
