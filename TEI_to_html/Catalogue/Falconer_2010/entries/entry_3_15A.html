LOCKYER WILLIS HART AND JAMES ATKINSON, Bameean. Tajuks of Bameean and Kohistan, c. 1840.
This view shows a Tajik family group standing in front of the cliffs of the Bamiyan valley, with the niche containing the large figure of Buddha on the far right. As well as managing to convey the sharp relief of the caves dotting the rock-face and the scale of the mountains in the distance, Atkinson took care in recording the costumes that were then worn in the central highlands of Afghanistan.
Lithograph, from James Atkinson and Lockyer Willis Hart, Character and Costumes of Afghaunistan, 2nd series (London: 1843), British Library.
