JAMES RATTRAY, Gool-Mahomeed Khaun, King of the Ghilgies, 1841.
After surrendering to the British in April 1841, the Ghilzai chief Gul Muhammad Khan was put under house arrest in Kabul, where Rattray sketched him in a caravanserai, seen on the right. Despite his fearsome reputation, Rattray found him very friendly.
Lithograph from James Rattray, Scenery, Inhabitants and Costumes, Afghaunistan (London, 1848), British Library.
