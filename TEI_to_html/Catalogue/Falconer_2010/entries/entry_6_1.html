After the British invasion of Afghanistan in late 1878 and the death of Sher Ali in early 1879, his son and successor Yaqub Khan negotiated a treaty at Gandamak, which permitted the British to install an envoy in Kabul. These men, wearing the kilt of the Highlanders, formed the escort for the Amir at these talks.
Albumen print, British Library.
