CHARLES MASSON, Remains of the Citadel of Ghulghul, 1832.
This drawing shows the fortified urban site of Shahr-i Ghulghula in the Bamiyan valley, which is believed to have been destroyed by the Mongols in 1221. Masson later wrote of the site that ‘the desolate spot itself has a peculiar solemnity, not merely from its lonely and startling evidences of past grandeur, but because nature appears to have invested it with a character of mystery and awe…’
Pen and ink drawing; British Library.
