JAMES RATTRAY, The Bullock’s Hump, and Military Cantonments, Kandahar, c. 1841-2.
The rocky outcrop to the west of the city of Qandahar was known to the British as the Bullock’s Hump. In the distance can be seen the British cantonment.
Lithograph from James Rattray, Scenery, Inhabitants and Costumes, Afghaunistan (London, 1848), British Library.
