CHARLES MASSON, Minars or Sultan Muhammad’s Columns, Ghizni, 1832.
At the time of Masson’s visit to Ghazni, the tall brick minarets of Bahram Shah and Mas’ud III, which date from 10th to 11th centuries, could still be accessed by means of internal stairs. This drawing represents an important record of the form of these unique monuments, of which only the lower sections now survive.
Pen and ink drawing; British Library.
