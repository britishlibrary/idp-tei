Or.6394/1
M.9
Hoernle 2: KT II, p. 65 (26); SD II/XXVI; SD, TV, p. 52

Section: 1
Order from the ṣṣau Phvaiṃ-hvuhi to the spāta Sīḍaka of Gaysāta (also SI P 94.24, 100.9). Hoernle, 1901, pl. vii, 1, no. 5. In KT V, p. 2 (4) and SD, TV, p. 52, this is referred to as “Or.6394.1” and “Or 6394,1 = H2.”

transliteration:
l.1: @ ṣṣau phvai-hvuhi tta parī gayseta spāta sīḍakä vara u ttyāṃ hvaṃ-
l.2: ḍāṃ vara cu pīęmīnā thauna puḍaṃda u thauna ni hauḍāṃda vañau va mara hārū są̄made u
l.3: hattäkaṃ mūri jistādä dasau ysācya drai-se ttyāṃ mūryau jsa kāṃha thaunaka gvaṣcāṃdä
l.4: drai-se paṃ-saya pęʾmīnai-y-ūṃ jsa thau gvaṣceṃ dirsä chā khu parau pvīrau tti mū-
l.5: ri haḍā haṃgi puṣa hauḍa haṃtsa ysaṃthina khu śau dva na ni dāśīdä himye
l.6: ttāṃjerä 10 mye haḍai ttā parau tsve SIGNUM

translation:
(1) The ṣṣau Phvai-hvuhi orders thus: To the spāta Sīḍaka in Gaysāta and to those men (2) who promised wool cloths and did not deliver the cloths. [note a: Cf. the situation in SI P 103.35, also about delivery of cloth.] Now, here for *you the merchant Są̄made and (3) Hattäkaṃ requested 10,300 mūrās. For those mūrās they (= those men?) had handed over 300 pieces of hemp cloth (4) worth 500 [= 15,000?]. I have handed over from them (?) thirty chās of woolen cloth.
When you hear the order, (5) at the expiry of the days, pay the mūrās fully together with interest. If they do not (pay) in full one (or) two it is OK.
(6) The order went out *to you on the 10th day of Ttuṃjārā.
