translation:
(1) 19th regnal year, on the 29th day of month of Braṃkhaysja.
The spāta Sīḍaka and the villagers all together have assigned the court (2) tax. [note a: Cf. Or.6395/2.2.] “Whole” paying men were 41, and “half-work” (men) [note b: hālairya is from *hālā-vīria- “half-work’er.” “Whole” men were “workmen”: Or.6401/2.2, M.T. i.17 (IOL Khot 44/5), D. v.4 (IOL Khot W 3), SI P 101.4 hālai hvaṃḍye ... kīrarai hvaṃḍye.] 12 children and old men. (3) They (were) *Puñaupīkṣa, Jsajsaka, Gaudaka, Cauṣaka, Stavala, {ī x de}, Kāśaka, Svarrnadätta, (4) Vāysaḍa, Upadatta, Valai, Jigeṃḍai, Īrasaṃga, Jsaysayuda, Vaśaʾrapuña.
A “whole” man (5) on state-work accordingly had (the following) *scheduled (work): [note c: On pravanai, the meaning of which is not clear, see KT IV, p. 70.] one man had to deliver 213 mūrās; and a “half” man 106 mūrās.
(6) Mūpadatta: 18 [“whole”?] men and a 7 “half” (men, which amount to) 1,500.
(7) Sābaka; 23 “whole” men and 5 “half-work” men, (which) amounts to 2,000 mūrās
(8) 433 mūrās

Note: The writing and reading of many of the numbers are uncertain and do not add up properly.
