translation:
... (r1) the stupa ... one should circumambulate it 77 times. And 77 times this (r2) dhāraṇī [and] mantra should be recited, and 77 times this dhāraṇī [and] mantra should be ordered to be written. One should be clean (and) well bathed. It should be written with great homage and while sitting in the middle of a well-cleaned maṇḍala. And the (r4) writer should give (be given?) a donation with seven jewels, and the writer should do homage with outstanding food and drink. And (r5) thus this kalparāja dhāraṇī should be written 77 times and be placed there in the middle of the caitya. Thus one should do homage (v1) to the caitya and clean it. What they do thus in that manner is expounded in this sutra.
The noble son or noble daughter whose life may be even completely destroyed (and) may have left *life, life comes back to him (or her), and he lives (v3) long, and he is delivered from all apāyas. And as for the previous āvaraṇas, on account of which he would have to go to an apāya, (v4) those are [extinguished?] for him. And for 77.000 lord buddhas (v5) [he will have accumulated] roots of goodness. [And] throughout (re)births he will be rid of all sins. And in all (re)births he *will be born remembering previous births.
