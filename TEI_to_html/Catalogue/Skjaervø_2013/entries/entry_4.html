Or.6393.2: KT V, p. 2 (3); D II/XXV: SD, TV, p. 52

Section: 1
Regnal year of Viśaʾ Vāhaṃ, year of the ṣṣau Ṣanīra. Buttąnaka is mentioned in docs. from (Viśaʾ Vāhaṃ’s) 20th (Or.6397/1 = Hoernle 7) and 21st (Hedin 4) years = 782-3. Hoernle, 1901, p. 37. PLATE 5b.

translation:
(1) [Of the -th regnal year] of Viśaʾ Vāhaṃ, king of Khotan, of the month of Kaja 22 days, in the year of the ṣṣau Ṣanīra.
This writ (2) (was made) for [this] reason that: Sīḍaka buys ground from Darmapuña in Veṃgulāṃ *from Braṃgāṃ (3) [... owed?] one kūsa 5 khas and one piece of cloth. Darmapuña has delivered. Sīḍaka has received (the land) (4) and two thaṃgas of cotton. Thus Sīḍaka has received this ground.
For a period of 6 autumns, if he has cultivated (only) one plot, [note a: B.: “if he makes the one field (?) to be planted” (SD, TV, p. 52, cf. p. 57); cf. Or.6397/1.5 kīra käʾstä īdä.] (then) (5) whenever Darmapuña has millet and cloth (and?) cotton he shall buy back the earth. Sīḍaka (6) can not *hinder [note b: Cf. LKhot. āhrrī < āhalj- “to control” (SD, TV, p. 57; Studies I, pp. 25-27)?] him.
This writ shall then take effect when Darmapuña places his finger on it.
Witness: (7) the parramai *Buttąnaka. Witness: *Japāṇaka. [note c: Japāṇaka- Or.6395/1.17, SI P 137.2.1, etc.] Witness: Surgāṃ from Birgaṃdara.
Darmapuña’s finger.
