Doc. concerning *tax-collectors (thaṃgaurāṃ pīḍaki?). 22nd year (of Väśaʾ Vāhaṃ = 789). The doc. contains provisions for sickness and loss of salary(?), but because of several lacunae and unknown words the sense of many of the provisions escapes us. Hoernle, 1901, p. 39.

translation:
(1) Year 22, 23 days of the month of Skarihvāra. This writ concerning *tax-payers (is) for the following reason:
(2) Viṃkausa is sending his brother german in lieu of tax (payment of) 5,000 (3) mūrās because Viṃkausa had promised *Sīña [note a: Cf. M.T. a.i.0033 (IOL Khot 41/1) paʾ sīña.] of Paʾ x thousand mūrās. (4) And all the villagers of Gaysāta took on this Mūpadatta in lieu of tax (5) 5,000 mūrās (for) ten months. And the villagers took that Mūpadatta in varāysai [note b: B. (SD, TV, p. 4) renders this word as “offer” in the combination kaʾrä varāysai “the offer of the gratuity,” but the expression kaʾrä N.N. in the wood tablets clearly contains the title and name of the scribe; see Studies III, pp. 39-40.] ... (6) received for tax, which the village haṃdasta, the spāta Sīḍaka ... varāysai (7) ... (But?) this was Mūpadatta’s ... They *received as tax.
(8) If he becomes sick he has seven days’ jurisdiction. And (if) he *remains feverish more (than that) then for him (9) ... work ... and *Mūpadatta himself provides shoes and ... (it). (10) And the spāta provides one garment for him. If that Mūpadatta leaves *still owing work (11) (and) brings a *complaint for (loss of?) day-works and (if) he *disregards the villagers, (then) they shall bring a *complaint for mūrās.
(12) And this writ concerning *tax-payers will take effect when Viṃkausa and Mūpadatta (13) put their fingers on it and the villagers all together put their fingers on it.
(14) These were the witnesses: the spāta Sudaṃ, the spāta Budesa.
(15-22) Brīyāsa’s finger (mark), Viṃkausa, Puñargaṃ, Mūpadatta, Maiyadatta, Japāṇaka, Arrtai, Arsäla, Śalā, Jsajsaka, [note c: Jsajsaka is a dānapati in Hedin 23.] Pheṃdūka, and Mahvetara’s finger (marks).
