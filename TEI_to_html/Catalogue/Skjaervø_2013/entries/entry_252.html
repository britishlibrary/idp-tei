Or.11344.4: KT II, p. 34 (5)

Section: 1
This is a variant draft(?) of the same document as Hedin 1 (KT IV, p. 55).

transliteration:
l.1: [30] 5 mye kṣuṇi cira kṣvā auvā kīrarā hvaṃ(ḍi) tcahauratcaʾhauʾsi śe hvaṃḍye peʾmīnai thau [20 3 chā]
l.2: tta nva hvaḍā thauna himārä 20 5 u 10 2 chā tsīṣī u hārvāṃ u tsā́tā[ṃ
l.3: thauna himārä hauda u dirsä chā vaña nva kṣauvā hauḍa thauna himya 20 3 u [śūdasi chā]
l.4: x [t]suna thauna dva hālai sūlya hauḍāṃdi kṣau na ra byaidi tti cu spāta śeʾmaki /
l.5: th[au]na 3 tsīṣī spāta sudārrjāṃ haurāña u spāta yąniviṭi śau tcaʾhauʾsä ch[ā pharṣa sāma]-
l.6: di thau puḍi 20 chā ṣanīraki 20 chā budarmä 20 3 chā hrrīviṭi u visarr[jā]ṃ /
l.7: mūrai ysārä kṣi-si tcahauʾsä yaudarä hīye vāra mūri dvī ysārä dvī-sa paṃjsāsä sūrada[t]t[i hīye]
l.8: vāra mūrä ysārä kṣi-si tcahausi paʾ sudatti vāra —

translation:
(1) In the *35th regnal year four workmen in Cira of the Six Towns [sold/bought?] forty-four per man (of) woolen cloth. (2) Those accordingly shall be 25 cloths and 12 chās for the men.
For the tsīṣī and the merchants and the *wealthy [note a: Cf. Or.11252/30.3.] ... (3) there shall be 7 cloths and 30 chās.
Now, according to the vouchers the cloths sold were 23 and ... (4) tsunas.
Two and a half cloths the *Sogdians have sold, though there are no vouchers yet (for them).
Those which the spāta Śeʾmaka [sold ...]
(5) 3 cloths shall be given to the tsīṣī, the spāta Sudārrjuṃ, and one to the spāta Yąniviṭi. One of fourteen chās ... [the pharṣa Sāma](6)da promised a cloth of 20 chās, Ṣanīraka 20 chās, Budarma 23 chās, Hrrīviṭi and ...
(7) 1,614 mūrās for it are owed by Yaudara. 2,215 mūrās are owed by Sūradatta. (8) 1,614 are owed by Sudatta from Paʾ.
