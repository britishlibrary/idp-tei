translation:
(1) Year 17, 5 days of the month of Skarhvāra, of the king of Khotan Viśaʾ Vāhaṃ.
At that time this writ (was made) (2) for the following reason: Sīḍaka is getting a man for *official work together with water and ground. However many years accordingly they request any *official matter (3) in the future, Sīḍaka provides it, (whether) much (or) little—as much as they request. And whatever (4) old ṣaṃgas (of grain are) owed—Brīyāsa and Budarśaṃʾ keeps the account of them. Sīḍaka has no dispute there.
(5) And now Brīyāsa has delivered grain to the Chinese himself. And when—the grain at the top of the year—Sīḍaka delivers that, (6) and (= then) Brīyāsa will himself bring *that much. And whatever clothing goes to (= is sent to?) him, that Sīḍaka does for him, and (7) no bisalū will have gone out. (In/for?) 30 days that goes out which has come in.
And this document will take effect [when] (8) Brīyāsa and Budarśaṃʾ place (their) finger(s) [on it].
(9) The following were the witnesses: Puñargaṃ, Khauśpaṃ, [note a: Also in SI M52.2, 7, P103.53.2 (Emmerick).] Hatkaṃ. — Budarśaṃʾ’s finger, Puñargaṃ’s finger.
(10) Now, whatever may be the *complaints of Brīyāsa, in the future Budarśaṃʾ keeps the account of it for him. (11) Sīḍaka has no dispute there.
