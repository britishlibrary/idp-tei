Or.6395/2
M.9
Hoernle 3: KT II, p. 65 (27) = Or.6395.2: KT V, p. 4 (7); SD II/XXVII; SD, TV, p. 54

Section: 1
Doc. concerning taxes dated in the 17th regnal year (of Väśaʾ Vāhaṃ = 784), year of the ṣau Ṣacū. The ṣau Ṣacū also appears in Or.6400/2.2 (no year) together with Pheṃdūka and Puñargaṃ and in Hedin 4 (21st year) together with Buttąnaka. Hoernle, 1901, pl. vii, 2, no. 8.

transliteration:
l.1: @ 10 7mye kṣāṇä ṣau ṣacū salya rrāha(ja) māśtä 10 7my[e] haḍai gaysāta-

l.2: ja auya cvātajä māśtū thaṃgä samauttāṃdä haurāka hvaṃʾḍä hä-
l.3: mya 50 5 thaṃgä ye paṃ-ysārä paṃ-se mūrä śe hva[ṃ]ḍy[e] mūrä 10 x
l.4: sa täña haṃkhīśaʾ ysāḍa hvaṃʾḍä 8 mara mūñaṃdā x x paʾjsa
l.5: hauparadärsä natca ʾau hvaṃḍä dasau haṃbaʾca hvaṃḍä hämya
l.6: 50 5 SQUIGGLE

translation:
(1) In the 17th regnal year, in the year of the ṣṣau Ṣacū, the 17th day of the month of Rrāhaja. (2) The villagers of Gaysāta have agreed on their tax [note a: Cf. Or.6396/2.2; on samev- samautta-, see KT IV, p. 118.] for the month of Cvātaja.
There were 55 paying men. (3) The tax was 5,500 mūrās, for one man 1,000 mūrās. (4) Only, in this count (were) 8 old men; here *dwelling ... (men) *in full strength [note b: B.: “from Paʾ .”] (were) (5) 37; ... men of the village (were) 10. All in all there were (6) 55 [= 8 + 37 + 10] men.
