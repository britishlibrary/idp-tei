translation:
... those men being ... of themselves ... become. They will fall into that drection, then, ... false roads (kumārga) in the end ... they will become horses (or) camels. (*99) They will carry burdens, they will load [onto them] many ... and they will not take pity on them. They will become very tired, exhausted, and *hurt, ... and distressed. 100
The Lady Dūlakā ordered (this text) to be written together with her son the knower of the Tripiṭaka, the monk Jayabhadra and together with her brothers, the spāta Sīḍaka [note a: The spāta Sīḍaka in Or.6393/1, etc.] and the others, and with her sisters.
When they die there they obtain births among men. They become the servants of another and are very ... suffering in inferiority. (*101) When their human life is ended (of) at death they are able to hear the name, vows, puṇyas, and blessings of that buddha ..., (then) they make happiness, in (their) mind bliss [arises, their heart?] *rejoices, all are happy, they ..., they rejoice always. (*102) Then when ... respect great quickly they will obtain [deliverance] from all woes and sufferings, always they will obtain happiness ...
