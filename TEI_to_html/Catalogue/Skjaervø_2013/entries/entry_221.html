translation:
(a1) We make a report to the gracious Lord thus: in the Six Towns, (we) the tsīṣī (2) Sudārrjāṃ and the merchants in the Six Towns and all the people. And whoever/whatever ... (3) we in the ... section are alive only by the merits of Your Lordship.
Whatever [...] (4) there deign be for you, Your Lordship *at all times ..., now, Your Lordship, I have many court works (ordered) by you. And with the stānaḍas [note a: B.: Tib. *ston-bla “teacher-superior” (KT IV, p. 119).] (5) ... I have three stānaḍas, but one of them is very old, (and) he just does not [do] court [works] ... (6) And your (men) there (with you) at the Inner Fort are requesting lyibaʾ [claim exemption?] and thus do not [do] court works. ... (7) ... deigns obtain the Lord’s mercy. And when for you the five sections ... (b1) ... those of ... *section.
On account of the court tax and on account of the state works I went to ... to *make a complaint(?). (2) ... in ... Now, I had already paid twice the price, (but) there is no *relief (?). So now ... to you ... court ... (3) ... I shall serve as(?) price together with the wife and the sons. In the future I shall serve the hīyaude. ... (4) ... in that town, they do not make people unhappy. The stānaḍas are so lawless. ... (5) I had already done the [state?] works. If I manage to send (it) in the future, he will protect me now. ...
