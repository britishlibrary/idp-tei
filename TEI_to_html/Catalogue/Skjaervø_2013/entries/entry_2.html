Section: 1
11th year, i.e., of Viśaʾ Vāhaṃ, year of the tsīṣīs (also Hedin 45) = 773. Hoernle, 1901, p. 37. The scribe was forgetful of his subscripts.

translation:
(1) Year 11, eight days of the month of Cvātaja, in the year of the Tsīṣīs.
(2) This writ is for the reason that Puñārrjāṃ [note a: Puñārrjāṃ and Puñargaṃ are both mentioned in a list of recipients of large sums of money in SI P 103.36, dated in the year of the ṣṣau Ṣacū (= Viśaʾ Vāhaṃ’s16th or 17th). Puñārrjāṃ is mentioned in D.viii.2 (IOL Khot 3/5) and SI P 98.10. Puñargaṃ is mentioned several times in BL and SI docs. According to SI P 103.6 he comes from Gaysāta.] is getting wheat (in the amount of) (3) seven kūsas—it belonged to Puñargaṃ—from the khaysmaṃjā. [note b: The meaning of this term, which occurs several times, is not known. In Or.8211/1456 dvī khaysmaje “two khaysmajās” are mentioned.] And in the month of Ttuṃjārā (4) he will deliver cloth: woolen (cloth) 11 chās in length and (5) 30 chās 2 tsunas in width, so that Sīḍaka receives the cloth in the month of Ttuṃjārā. (6) And if he (Sīḍaka) should not receive it, (then) for a five-month period he (Puñārrjāṃ) shall pay one chā interest.
(7) And this writ will take effect when Puñārrjāṃ (8) places (his) finger on it.
(9) Puñārjāṃ’s finger (mark).
