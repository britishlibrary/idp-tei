Section: v1
Vernacular “Rhapsody on Yen-tzu” 晏子賦壹首 (head title). Same text as T244–245 but overlooked for collation by the editors. Begins in middle of a sentence of previous text on ms and breaks off abruptly at T245.2.2(方) to start practice writing 之 而 人 見 and other simple characters, mostly by whole rows. Interspersed with other practice characters and Buddhist homilies, all in very crude hands but mostly of one individual. Also copies the opening of the Thousand Character Primer by whole rows of each character for practice. Irregularly ruled. Copyist may have been someone who was just learning about Buddhism, for the secular texts are followed and preceded by simple Buddhist phrases and rules for living. Last lines are a lay society circular 社司轉帖右緣 (heading only, upside-down) and a circular relating to the yearly expenses of irrigation ditchmen 渠人轉帖右緣年支. There is no question that the copyist was someone in the process of becoming literate. Among the practice characters are references to a lay society, to monks 比丘, and to commoners 白衣.


Section: r1
Portion of the Lotus sūtra on faintly ruled, golden-yellow paper of very fine quality and in an excellent hand. Both beginning and end are damaged.
