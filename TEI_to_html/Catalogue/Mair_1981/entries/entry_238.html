Or.8210/S.173

Section: r1
Letters between Li Ling and Su Wu. Not used in collation of T848–850. Beige paper; mediocre hand. Colophon (in same hand as main text): “Written and inscribed by Chang Ying-chün, lay student at the Trailokya (‘Three Realms’) Monastery, on the eighth day of the sixth month in the year i-hai (975?).” 乙亥年六月八日三界寺學士郎張英俊書記之也. Colophon in different ink and hand: “Inscription of writing by ‘Iron-boy’ of the Trailokya Monastery.” 三界寺[#96](=鐵?)子書記. Twentieth-century notation on r°: “#703 [commercial numerals in red ink]; seems in the latter part to record letters between Li Ling and Su Wu. Chi-hsin(→hai) years were checked and found not to have this reign title.” 似後錄李陵与蘇武來往書信己辛(→亥)查無此年號.


Section: v1
Lay society circular announcing a spring meeting written in an extremely crude hand. Practice characters and sentences, including “Long live Secretary Buddy!” 尚書阿郎萬歲.
