Section: r1
“Lament of the Woman of Ch’in,” one scroll (head and end titles). By the Omissioner of the Right, {Wei Chuang} 秦婦吟一卷 右補闕艹(badly torn). Poor to mediocre hand; red punctuation; corrections. Colophon (same hand): “Copied and inscribed on the seventeenth of the second month in the year 957 by the domestic lay student, Ma Fu-te. If you write with a poor hand, and if there are definite mistakes, when a famous copyist sees it, you’ll get five firm lashes.” 顯徳二(→四)年丁巳歲二月十七[日]就家學士郎馬富徳書記 手[#75](=若)笔[#76](=惡) [#75]有决錯 名書見者 决丈(→杖)五[#77](=索). Additional colophon (different hand): “Inscription of copying by the boy lay student, Ma Fu-te, on the nineteenth day of the second month of the year 957.” 大周顯徳四年丁巳歲二月十九日學士童兒馬富徳書記. Scribbles amongst the colophons.

Colophon from r° repeated twice for practice. Among practice writing, this title: “Madame Ts’ui Trains a Girl” 崔氏夫人訓女文. Colophon: “Inscription of copying on the fifteenth day of the fifth month in the year 976 by the lay student, Yang Ting-ch’ien.” 丙子年五月十五日斈仕郎楊定遷自手書記之耳.
