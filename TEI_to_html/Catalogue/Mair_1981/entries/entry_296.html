Section: r1
“Transformation Text on Mahāmaudgalyāyana Rescuing His Mother from the Underworld,” with Pictures, one scroll, with preface 大目乾連冥間救母變文幷圖(sic)一卷并序 (head title). “Transformation Text on Mahāmaudgalyāyana,” one scroll 大目犍連變文一卷 (end title). Main text for T714–744. Beige paper; lightly ruled; much used and worn. Mediocre, hurried hand; still some corrections even though the numerous extant compatible mss prove that this was already a relatively fixed text. Colophon (same hand, but more deliberate): “Written on the sixteenth day of the fourth month in the year 921 by Hsüeh An-chün, lay student of the Pure Land Monastery.” 貞明柒年辛巳歲四月十六日淨土寺學郎薛[#119](=安)俊寫. Some more writing just below this is no longer legible. Hsüeh An-chün was also the copyist of Pelliot chinois 2054. I discovered this connection by chance in 1974 and have recently been able to satisfy myself that the handwriting on the two mss is identical. Additional colophon (written by the copyist): “Chang Pao-ta’s ms” 張保達文書. This can not mean that the text was composed by Chang Pao-ta, as Giles asserts. Cf. Pelliot chinois 2049. Another line of a different text follows but is unreadable because it has been torn off straight down the whole width of the scroll. No trace of pictures. Has identifying pien-wen formula.


Section: v1
List of monks in the Pure Land Monastery. Practice writing, including this: “As for doing evil, no mention is made of what has been done by heaven.” 行[#120]不論天所造 (crossed out with light ink and followed by “crime” 罪 in same ink). Head title of r° repeated thrice in a very clumsy hand. Chang Pao-ta’s name is written after the first.
