“Brahmacārin Wang,” one scroll 王梵志一卷 (end title). Coarse, beige paper. Respectable hand; partially punctuated in red. Colophon (very different hand, much cruder): “Copied by the official secretary, ‘Confucian Sage’ Wu, in the twelfth month of the year 937/8, continuing himself from the beginning of Fan Fu-ch’uan” 清泰四年丁酉歲十二月舍書吳儒賢從頭自續氾富川. In a nearly illegible note following the colophon, Fan Fu-ch’uan is described as being a “commoner of Hung-kuan (in Honan)” 洪[#121](=關?)鄉(?)百姓.
