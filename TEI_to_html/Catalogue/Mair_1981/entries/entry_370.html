“Tale of the Capture of Chi Pu,” one scroll 捉(this character written in lighter, greyish ink)季布傳文一卷 (head title). “Lyric Text of Chi Pu Cursing at the Battlefront in the Third Year of the Great Han Dynasty,” one scroll 大漢三年季布罵陣詞文一卷 (end title). Used in collation of T51–71. Colophon: “‘Chi Pu,’ one scroll, written in this own hand by ‘Slaveboy’ Yin, lay student of Secretary Fan, on the tenth day of the fourth month of the year 978.” 太平興[國]三年戊寅歲四月十日𠖰孔目學仕郎陰奴兒自手寫季布一卷. For “Slaveboy Yin,” see also Pelliot chinois 3910. Additional note, partially erased but reconstructed: “{Donkey Yin} sang incorrectly” {陰}{驢}子錯唱. This note is based on the last line of the text (T71.4b): “Don’t say that the lyricist has sung untruly” 莫道詞人唱不眞. N.B.: This final line and the end title are in a good hand. All the rest is in a poor, unpracticed hand, including the colophon and the additional note. Followed by Brahmacārin Wang’s poems. Coarse, brownish beige paper. Childish practice characters and numbers in margins; doodles throughout. Erasures, corrections. A very messy, very crude ms; clearly the work of a beginning student. The cover of this copybook is full of scribbles, some of which are very faint and extremely difficult to decipher: “Wu-yin year (978). The one who writes the later inscriptions is a chronicler (?).
