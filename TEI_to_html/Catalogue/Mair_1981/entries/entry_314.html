Section: v1
“Nidāna Transformation on a Maiden in the Women’s Palace of King Bimbisāra [Named] ‘Intends-to-Create-Merit’ Who Is Reborn in Heaven for Having Given Her Devotion to a Stūpa” 頻婆娑羅王后宮綵女功德意供養塔生天因緣變 (head title). “Nidāna on ‘Intends-to-Create-Merit’ Who Is Reborn in Heaven for Having Given Her Devotion to a Stūpa” 功德意供養塔生天緣 (title after introduction, at T765.9). Used to make the first part of the composite text on T764–769 (ends at 767.2). Lacks identifying pien-wen pre-verse formula except once in the concluding portion which is on Pelliot chinois 3051; should be classed as a mixed nidāna. Begins (764.2–765.5) with the same seat-settling text as that (344.3–345.7) which precedes the prosimetric narrative of the destruction of demons later on this same scroll. An important distinction, however, is that it lacks the identifying ya-tso-wen formula of the latter. In its place, it has a short prose bridge leading from the introduction (seat-settling text minus sūtra title quotation formula) to the nidāna proper. The prosimetric narrative of the destruction of demons (lacks titles at head and end) follows immediately after the first piece on v° is abandoned. Used in collation of T344.3(年)–345.5, 345.7, 346.9–354.10. After this point, the present ms — which continues with an extract from a sūtra about an Indian king — is completely different from Pelliot chinois 2187 which addressed itself to topical matters. The parts of the various speakers are marked almost as though this were a playscript. All texts on v° have the same distinctive angular style of calligraphy as r° although it varies from quite small to rather large. Few corrections, additions, and erasures throughout.


Section: r1
“Stanzas on Various Types of Behavior,” one scroll 百行章一卷 (actual title). The name of the author, Tu Cheng-lun 杜正倫, is given just under the title. Coarse paper; lightly ruled.
