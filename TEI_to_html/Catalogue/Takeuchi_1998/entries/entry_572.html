Frag. of two folios from concertina. Each folio has four lines of text ruled in red ink on both sides. Similar in style to Text 571. Recto is a passage near the end ofGser-'od dam-pa'i rnga-sgraand corresponds with minor differences to parallel Dunhuang texts (e.g.,LVP_1962: 449: 27-33,66: 7–14). It continues to Text 574 recto. Texts 575 and 576 are pieces from the same text, to which Texts 571 and 577 may belong. Verso, written in a more cursive style than recto, is another Buddhist text (unidentified) and is probably a continuation from Text 574 verso. V1 mentionsdbu-skra dmar"red hair" andspyan-gsum"three eyes."
