Badly damaged paper with many holes; several holes seem to have been cut intentionally. Writing on both sides is faint and not clearly legible. Recto seems to be a contract for the sale of a male bondservant. R1 gives the date "[In the spring?] of the sheep year when a Khotanese officialKhri-gda'mand aspyan-officialDpal-bzher, etc., convoked an assembly at'U-ten." This indicates that the document was drawn up in the Khotan area. (Khri-gda'mmight be same asKhri-gdas;Thomas_1955: 44). The buyer's name is partly legible asZha-pong(r2). The seller's name is missing on recto, but there is a heading to the contract on verso (v5) which reads "The bond ofKhrom-skyes," suggesting thatKhrom-skyesis the seller. V1–v4 may be a continuation from recto, but is hardly legible. A few short lines near the recto's top edge may be the lower portions of some characters. Cf. Text 676 for the requisition number.
