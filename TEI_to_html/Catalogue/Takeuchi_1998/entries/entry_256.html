After greetings (r2), the sender, a horse-attendant, seems to speak of troubles (or family reasons) which hindered his work (r3–r6); a lawsuit was filed on his behalf by the lady's servants (r6–r7). R10–r11 say that the wage ofMe-nu nya-gzigs, a subject ofzhangRgyal-bzher, was raised from sevenzhoto fourteen.
