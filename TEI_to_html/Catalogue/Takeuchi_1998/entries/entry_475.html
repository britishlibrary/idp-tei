Thirteen small frags. were placed together under the site number M. I. ix. 12. Ten of them (here numbered M. I. ix. 12. a. – j.: Texts 475–483) seem to be pieces from the same ms., probably a letter. Probably names of addressees are partly visible in Text 475 (r1). Text 480 mentionsdbon-sras"grandsons and sons(i.e., descendants) [of the Btsanpo]". Texts 479 and 480 contain writing on verso, most probably the form of address. Frags. i. and j. (Text 483) have been joined together.
