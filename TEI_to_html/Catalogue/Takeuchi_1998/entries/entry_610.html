Two small frags. of M. I. xxviii. 005. a. have been fit into an area in the middle of M. I. xxviii. 004., making up the missing part of r5–r7; the reconstructed text is given here. It used to form one piece (probably a roll) with Text 611 that bears the same site number; Text 610 recto is the left page and Text 611 recto is the right. Text 611 recto can partly be identified as theLaṅkāvatāra-sūtra, but Text 610 recto does not correspond with the sūtra. Another Buddhist text, probably a Tantra, written in a more cursive style, is on verso, where Text 610 is the right page and Text 611 is the left. Verso mentionsrdo-rje pad-ma'i ting-'dzing(v2),las rnam bzhi(v4),'das-logs(v18).
