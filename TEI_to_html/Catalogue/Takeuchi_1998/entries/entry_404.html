a folio from concertina, complete

Oblong sheet; most probably one folio from concertina. Recto (N.B.: recto and verso were mistakenly reversed in Plates) is the middle part of theGtsug-tor gdugs dkar-po, corresponding to Dunhuang texts: e.g., P[elliot] 23, fol.19, l.3–fol.20, l.4; P 83, column 2, 4–8 (also cf. Peking, nos. 202–205). One line of additional writing appears in the margin below r5, and a phrase is written vertically in the right margin, both by the same hand but different from the main text. Verso is another Buddhist text by the same hand as recto main text. The phrasernam-dag-zhIng der skye-bar 'gyur"will be born in the pure land" is repeated five times.
