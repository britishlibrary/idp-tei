Texts 567 and 568 are pieces of the same ms. According to Francke, they used to bear the same site number M. I. xvi. 0011. R1 and r2 in Text 568, respectively, are likely to be the continuation of r2 and r3 in Text 567. But these two cannot be joined together. Due to paper damage, the text is very fragmentary; but it may be a sort of land register listing the place names and field owners.Te[n?] khang-rngyingandPe-ta-phyemay be the place names. R4 in Text 568 reads "the field of one [. . .] is atPe-ta-phyi." Only relatively clear and decipherable parts are given here.
