Three frags. bearing the same site number (here distinguished by adding a., b., and c.) are pieces of the same ms. with writing on both sides apparently by the same hand. Frag. a. was mistakenly joined to frag. b. (as shown in Plates) and should be reversed so that the the first two lines of a. recto will be joined with b. r2 and r3. This newly reconstructed text is given here. Both recto and verso seem to be parts of a letter. Personal names are not clearly legible;]yu(r1) may be the end of a name; r3 seems to contain[jo]-coLha-dpal. Published as Text C inStein_1907.
