Text 611 bears the same site number as Text 610. They used to form one piece (probably a roll); Text 611 recto is the right page and Text 610 recto is the left. R4–r8 can be identified as the middle part of theLaṅkāvatāra-sūtra, corresponding to Lhasa ed. 149b2–6; but the remaining part is not the sūtra. Needs further comparison with other relevant texts: e.g.,LVP_1962: 167andLVP_1962: 219;PT_608andPT_609;Q_776.sam-gtanis spelled asbsam-brtan(r5). Another Buddhist text, prabably a Tantra, written in a more cursive style, is on verso, where Text 611 is the left page and Text 610 is the right. Text 611 (v3–v4) mentions four continents:'Dzam-ling-dag,Lus-spags-po,Ban-glang-spyod, andDgra-myi-snyan. V7–v9 explains two mystic syllables,omandhum.Rnam-par snang-mdzad(Vairocana) appears in v20.
