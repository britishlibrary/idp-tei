Recto is probably a contract for hiring horses. Right and lower edges are torn off; writing indistinct and not clearly legible in parts. R1 gives the date of contract: "In the spring of the ox year, whenblonBtsan-sum-bzher,blonMang-zigs,blonDpal-bzang, and so on [convoked] the assembly atDru-gu;" r3 mentions "a spare(?) horse ofRlang 'brug-legswas hired." Verso is a letter addressed toblonStag-bzangfrom'Brug-legs, probably identical withRlang 'brug-legson recto. Writing is faint and indistinct in parts. Francke suggest that both recto and verso refer to an infectious disease among horses.
