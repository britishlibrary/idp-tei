Three frags. (Texts 622–624) bear the same site number M. I. xxxii. 5. h. According to Francke, they were once held together by red cord. Now separated, they are apparently pieces of the same ms. Texts 622 and 623 bear writing on both sides. Verso, consisting of mantras, is written in the upright sutra style, and recto in a more cursive style. For Text 624, only a few unconnected syllables, such assnaandshe, are legible on verso, which apparently belong with the versos of Texts 622 and 623. Recto writing seems faded. The three frags. cannot be joined together. Since their relative position can be inferred, the reconstructed text is given here. They also seem to belong to the same ms. as Text 621.
