Recto is a complete contract, as described inTakeuchi_1995. It was written after the verso document was discarded. Verso is the lower part of probably a letter. The upper edge is cut off. Text is unfinished. Consecutive dates such as, "16th, 17th, 18th, 20th, 23rd, 27th days," can be made out. Since writing is extremely faint, the reading given here remains tentative. Acquired by Stein through Badruddīn Khān together with Sanskrit and Khotanese texts during his third expedition. The Sanskrit and Khotanese mss. (Khad. 04-20, 038-051) are listed inStein_1928: 1021,Stein_1928: 1022,Stein_1928: 1027, but there is no mention of Khad. 052.
