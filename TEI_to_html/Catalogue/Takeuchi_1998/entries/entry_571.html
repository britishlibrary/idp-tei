concertina, frag (T, L, R, B)

Frag. of two folios from concertina. Each folio has four lines of text ruled in red ink on both sides; red vertical lines mark the right and left margins of the text. Writing on recto is in an upright sutra style, while verso is in a more cursive style. Recto resembles and possibly belongs with Texts 572, 574–576 recto, which contain part ofGser-'od dam-pa'I rnga-sgra. But no corresponding passages found between Text 571 recto and parallel Dunhuang texts (e.g.,LVP_1962: 449,PT_66). Verso is another Tantric Buddhist text. It speaks of relation and correspondence betweenTrikāya,Trikarma,Trikāla, five causes of mental misery, and five Buddhas, i.e.,Snang-ba mtha-yas(Amitābha),Akṣobhya,Rnam-par snang-mdzad(Vairocana),Amogasiddhi, andRin-can 'byung-ldan(Ratnasaṁbhava). Cf. Text 577 verso.
