A complete Tibetan document written on the back side of a Khotanese text. The text mostly consists of a name list, where sixteen seemingly Khotanese priests are named under a unit [headed by] Great priestStagyi rgyal-mtshan(r2–r6). They seem to have been responsible for paying barley duty imposed on the unit. The barley serves as a fee for caring for an official,blonRgyal-bzang, while he was ill (r1–r2). A priest,Nog-su ber-zha, collected and forwarded the barley (r6–r7). This interpretation, however, is tentative. Note several uncommon spellings, e.g.,stagyi(r2) andtsadzugo(r6). Both the Tibetan and Khotanese texts have been published inHoernle_1916; Khotanese text inBailey_1945-1963: 2:68. No site number is indicated on the ms. The Khotanese text suggests its provenance to be the area of "Six Towns" east of Khotan.
