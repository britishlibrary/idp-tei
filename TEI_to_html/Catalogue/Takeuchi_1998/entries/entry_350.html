One line of Tibetan, a scribal exercise of the forms of address, is written in the bottom margin of a Chinese Buddhist text. Note that the sender's name precedes that of the addressee's. Verso is a Khotanese Buddhist text (seeBailey_1945-1963: 3:128–129. Ms. was originally part of a long scroll together with Kha. i. 221; Stein states, "As if to illustrate further the polyglot character of Khotan Buddhism, a line in Tibetan, too, appears at the bottom of the smaller of the two roll pieces, Kha. i. 221." (Stein_1921: 164,Stein_1921: 1445). The Chinese text, described as C 47 in Appendix toLVP_1962: 251, is part of the大般涅槃經(Mahā-parinirvāṇa sūtra), vol. 9:T_374: vol. 12: 4209a10–b10.
